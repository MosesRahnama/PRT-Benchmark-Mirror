**Assessment of the helper theorems**

The file defines a structural measure `kappa : Trace → Nat` that is used to argue termination of the rewrite calculus.  
Two lemmas are introduced to relate the `kappa`‑value of the `recDelta` constructor (the “recursive” case) to the `kappa`‑value of the term that appears after a rewrite step.

Below we verify each lemma purely by unfolding the definition of `kappa` and using elementary properties of `Nat.max`.

---

### 1. `kappa_rec_delta_step`

```lean
theorem kappa_rec_delta_step (b s n : Trace) :
  kappa (recDelta b s (delta n)) = kappa (recDelta b s n) + 1
```

**Unfolding `kappa`**

* For a term of the form `recDelta b s (delta n)` the definition gives  

  \[
  \kappa\bigl(\text{recDelta}\;b\;s\;(\delta n)\bigr)
  = \max\bigl(\max(\kappa b,\kappa s),\kappa n\bigr) + 1 .
  \]

* For a term of the form `recDelta b s n` (where the third argument is *not* a `delta`) the definition gives  

  \[
  \kappa\bigl(\text{recDelta}\;b\;s\;n\bigr)
  = \max\bigl(\max(\kappa b,\kappa s),\kappa n\bigr) .
  \]

Hence

\[
\kappa\bigl(\text{recDelta}\;b\;s\;(\delta n)\bigr)
= \bigl(\max\bigl(\max(\kappa b,\kappa s),\kappa n\bigr)\bigr) + 1
= \kappa\bigl(\text{recDelta}\;b\;s\;n\bigr) + 1 .
\]

The equality follows directly from the two clauses of `kappa`. No additional hypotheses are needed, so the lemma is **sound**.

### 2. `kappa_rec_succ_drop`

```lean
theorem kappa_rec_succ_drop (b s n : Trace) :
  kappa (app s (recDelta b s n)) < kappa (recDelta b s (delta n))
```

**Step‑by‑step calculation**

1. **Left‑hand side** (`app` case)

   By definition of `kappa` for `app`:

   \[
   \kappa\bigl(\text{app}\;s\;(\text{recDelta}\;b\;s\;n)\bigr)
   = \max\bigl(\kappa s,\;\kappa(\text{recDelta}\;b\;s\;n)\bigr).
   \]

   Using the definition of `kappa` for `recDelta b s n` (non‑`delta` case),

   \[
   \kappa(\text{recDelta}\;b\;s\;n)
   = M \quad\text{where}\quad
   M := \max\bigl(\max(\kappa b,\kappa s),\kappa n\bigr).
   \]

   Since `M` already dominates `\kappa s` (it is a `max` that includes `\kappa s`), we have

   \[
   \max\bigl(\kappa s, M\bigr) = M .
   \]

   Therefore

   \[
   \kappa\bigl(\text{app}\;s\;(\text{recDelta}\;b\;s\;n)\bigr) = M .
   \]

2. **Right‑hand side** (`recDelta` with a `delta` argument)

   By the `recDelta`‑`delta` clause:

   \[
   \kappa\bigl(\text{recDelta}\;b\;s\;(\delta n)\bigr)
   = M + 1 .
   \]

3. **Inequality**

   Combining the two results:

   \[
   \kappa\bigl(\text{app}\;s\;(\text{recDelta}\;b\;s\;n)\bigr) = M
   \quad\text{and}\quad
   \kappa\bigl(\text{recDelta}\;b\;s\;(\delta n)\bigr) = M + 1 .
   \]

   Since for natural numbers `M < M + 1` holds by definition of `<`, we obtain

   \[
   \kappa\bigl(\text{app}\;s\;(\text{recDelta}\;b\;s\;n)\bigr)
   < \kappa\bigl(\text{recDelta}\;b\;s\;(\delta n)\bigr) .
   \]

Thus the inequality is a direct consequence of the `+1` added in the `delta` case and the monotonicity of `Nat.max`. No extra assumptions are required, so the lemma is **sound**.

Both helper theorems are provably correct given the current definition of `kappa`:

* `kappa_rec_delta_step` is an exact rewriting of the two `kappa` clauses for `recDelta`.
* `kappa_rec_succ_drop` follows from the fact that `kappa (app …)` collapses to the same maximum `M` that appears in the `recDelta` clause, while the `delta` version of `recDelta` adds a strict `+1`.

Consequently, the helper strategy that uses these lemmas to show a strict decrease of the `kappa` measure across rewrite steps is **sound**. No contradictions or hidden side‑conditions appear, and the calculations confirm the intended termination argument.
