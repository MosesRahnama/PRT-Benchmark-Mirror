### Key Observation: The Measure Design

The critical design choice is a **split case** in `kappa`:
```lean
| .recDelta b s (.delta n) => Nat.max (Nat.max (kappa b) (kappa s)) (kappa n) + 1
| .recDelta b s n         => Nat.max (Nat.max (kappa b) (kappa s)) (kappa n)
```

The first case adds `+1` when the third argument is concretely `.delta n`. This setup is intentional: it makes `recDelta` terms with a delta-structured third argument "heavier" than the general case.

### Soundness Assessment

**The helper strategy is sound as written.** Both lemmas should be provable:

1. **First lemma** relies on Lean's pattern matching semantics: the concrete term `.delta n` matches the special case, while the variable `n` in `recDelta b s n` falls to the general case—making their `kappa` values differ by exactly 1.

2. **Second lemma** exploits that the measure of the reduct (`app s (recDelta b s n)`) is the max of three values, which equals the three-way max in the redex, but strictly less than when that max is incremented by 1.

The `R_rec_succ` rule correctly decreases the measure, establishing a local termination argument for that reduction.

**Note:** This `kappa` measure alone doesn't prove global termination—most other rules either preserve or could increase the measure. But for the targeted rule, the strategy is correct.
