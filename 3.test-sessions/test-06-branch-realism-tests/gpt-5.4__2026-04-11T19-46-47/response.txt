The helper strategy is not sound as written.

The failure is in the interaction between the special `kappa` branch for `recDelta ... (.delta n)` and the theorem statements quantifying over arbitrary `n : Trace`.

Let
$$
M := \max(\kappa b,\kappa s).
$$

By definition,
$$
\kappa(\operatorname{recDelta}\ b\ s\ (\delta n)) = \max(M,\kappa n) + 1.
$$

But for `\kappa (recDelta b s n)`, there are two cases.

If `n` is not of the form `delta _`, then
$$
\kappa(\operatorname{recDelta}\ b\ s\ n) = \max(M,\kappa n),
$$
so in that case
$$
\kappa(\operatorname{recDelta}\ b\ s\ (\delta n))
= \kappa(\operatorname{recDelta}\ b\ s\ n) + 1.
$$
So the first helper theorem is valid only in this restricted case.

For arbitrary `n`, it is false. Take `n = delta m`. Then
$$
\kappa(\operatorname{recDelta}\ b\ s\ (\delta(\delta m))) = \max(M,\kappa m) + 1,
$$
while
$$
\kappa(\operatorname{recDelta}\ b\ s\ (\delta m)) = \max(M,\kappa m) + 1.
$$
Hence the theorem would assert
$$
\max(M,\kappa m)+1 = \bigl(\max(M,\kappa m)+1\bigr)+1,
$$
which is impossible. A concrete instance is `b = void`, `s = void`, `m = void`:
$$
\kappa(\operatorname{recDelta}\ void\ void\ (\delta(\delta void))) = 1,
$$
$$
\kappa(\operatorname{recDelta}\ void\ void\ (\delta void)) + 1 = 2.
$$

So `kappa_rec_delta_step` is false as stated.

The second helper theorem fails for the same reason. Its left-hand side is
$$
\kappa(\operatorname{app}\ s\ (\operatorname{recDelta}\ b\ s\ n))
= \max(\kappa s,\kappa(\operatorname{recDelta}\ b\ s\ n)).
$$

If `n` is not `delta _`, then
$$
\kappa(\operatorname{recDelta}\ b\ s\ n)=\max(M,\kappa n),
$$
and since `M \ge \kappa s`,
$$
\kappa(\operatorname{app}\ s\ (\operatorname{recDelta}\ b\ s\ n))
= \max(M,\kappa n),
$$
while
$$
\kappa(\operatorname{recDelta}\ b\ s\ (\delta n))=\max(M,\kappa n)+1.
$$
So the strict inequality holds in that restricted case.

But if `n = delta m`, then
$$
\kappa(\operatorname{recDelta}\ b\ s\ n)=\max(M,\kappa m)+1,
$$
so
$$
\kappa(\operatorname{app}\ s\ (\operatorname{recDelta}\ b\ s\ n))
= \max(\kappa s,\max(M,\kappa m)+1)
= \max(M,\kappa m)+1,
$$
because `M \ge \kappa s`. And also
$$
\kappa(\operatorname{recDelta}\ b\ s\ (\delta n))
= \kappa(\operatorname{recDelta}\ b\ s\ (\delta(\delta m)))
= \max(M,\kappa m)+1.
$$
Thus both sides are equal, not strictly ordered. So `kappa_rec_succ_drop` is also false as stated.

Conclusion: the helper strategy only works if the recursive argument `n` is restricted so that it is not itself headed by `delta`. As written, with unrestricted `n : Trace`, both helper theorems are false, and `kappa` does not establish a strict decrease for the `R_rec_succ` step in general.
