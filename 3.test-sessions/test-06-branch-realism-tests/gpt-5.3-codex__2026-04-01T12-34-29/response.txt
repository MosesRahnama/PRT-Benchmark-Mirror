Verdict: the helper strategy is not sound as written.

The key issue is that both helper theorems near
and
are false in full generality.

1. First helper theorem is not valid for all n

Claim:
$$
\kappa(\mathrm{recDelta}\ b\ s\ (\delta n))=\kappa(\mathrm{recDelta}\ b\ s\ n)+1
$$

From the definition:
- $\kappa(\mathrm{recDelta}\ b\ s\ (\delta n))=M+1$, where $M=\max(\max(\kappa b,\kappa s),\kappa n)$.
- But $\kappa(\mathrm{recDelta}\ b\ s\ n)$ depends on whether n itself is of the form $\delta m$.

If $n=\delta m$, then:
$$
\kappa(\mathrm{recDelta}\ b\ s\ n)=\kappa(\mathrm{recDelta}\ b\ s\ (\delta m))=M+1
$$
so RHS becomes $(M+1)+1=M+2$, while LHS is $M+1$.

Concrete counterexample:
- $b=\mathrm{void}, s=\mathrm{void}, n=\delta\ \mathrm{void}$
- LHS: $\kappa(\mathrm{recDelta}\ \mathrm{void}\ \mathrm{void}\ (\delta(\delta\ \mathrm{void})))=1$
- RHS: $\kappa(\mathrm{recDelta}\ \mathrm{void}\ \mathrm{void}\ (\delta\ \mathrm{void}))+1=1+1=2$

So theorem 1 is false.

2. Second helper theorem also fails in general

Claim:
$$
\kappa(\mathrm{app}\ s\ (\mathrm{recDelta}\ b\ s\ n))<\kappa(\mathrm{recDelta}\ b\ s\ (\delta n))
$$

Using the same counterexample $b=s=\mathrm{void}, n=\delta\ \mathrm{void}$:
- RHS: $\kappa(\mathrm{recDelta}\ \mathrm{void}\ \mathrm{void}\ (\delta(\delta\ \mathrm{void})))=1$
- LHS:
$$
\kappa(\mathrm{app}\ \mathrm{void}\ (\mathrm{recDelta}\ \mathrm{void}\ \mathrm{void}\ (\delta\ \mathrm{void})))
=\max(0,1)=1
$$
Hence $1<1$ is false.

Interpretation for termination intent

The failure comes from this interaction:
- $\kappa(\delta t)=\kappa(t)$ (delta is measure-neutral),
- recDelta gets a +1 bonus when its third argument is syntactically delta-shaped.

After one succ-step, the recursive call may still have a delta-shaped third argument, preserving that +1 and preventing strict descent.

So the helper-based decrease argument for the rec-succ rule is not sound in its current quantified form.
