The proposed measure is not sound as a lexicographically decreasing termination measure for Step in KO7-LLM-Benchmark/test-files/Test-04-Measure-Verification.lean. The problem is the interaction between R_rec_zero, phase, and measure. The left side of R_rec_zero is always of the form recDelta b s void, so its phase is 0. But the right side is b, and b can itself have phase 1 if it is of the form recDelta _ _ (delta _). That makes the first component increase, which breaks lexicographic descent immediately.

A concrete counterexample is to take b = recDelta void void (delta void). Then
measure (recDelta b s void) = (0, 1 + cost b + cost s),
while
measure b = (1, cost b).
Under the usual lexicographic order on Nat × Nat, (1, cost b) is larger than (0, 1 + cost b + cost s), so the rewrite step recDelta b s void -> b is not decreasing. All the other rules do decrease under the proposed measure, but one counterexample is enough: as stated, the measure cannot certify termination.
