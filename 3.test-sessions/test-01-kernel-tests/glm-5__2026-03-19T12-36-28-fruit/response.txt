## Raw Assessment

**Yes, strong normalization can be established trivially.**

### Key Observation

The `Step` relation is defined as an inductive predicate with exactly 8 constructors—there is **no contextual closure**. This means:

- `Step (mango (grape t)) plum` holds
- But `Step (grape (mango (grape t))) (grape plum)` does **not** hold

Reductions can only occur at the **root** of a term, never under constructors.

### Consequence

Every term either:
1. **Matches exactly one rule** → reduces in one step to a normal form, or
2. **Matches no rule** → is already a normal form

For example:
- `R_apple_orange`: `banana b s (grape n) → pear s (banana b s n)` — the result is a normal form because `pear` has no rule and we cannot reduce under it
- `R_cherry_diff`: `cherry a b → mango (peach a b)` — normal form (no rule for `mango` with a `peach` argument)
- All other rules produce `plum` or a subterm, both normal forms

### Proof Roadmap

Define `Normal : Trace → Prop` by case analysis on the outermost constructor, excluding the 8 reducible patterns. Then prove:

```lean
theorem step_to_normal : ∀ t t', Step t t' → Normal t'
```

This establishes that every reduction produces a normal form in exactly one step. Strong normalization follows immediately—maximum reduction chain length is 1.

No structural axioms, ordinals, or external assumptions needed. The calculus as presented is already terminating by construction.
