## Analysis:

**Syntax**: 7 constructors (plum, grape, mango, peach, pear, banana, cherry) forming first-order terms.

**Critical observations**:
1. Most rules strictly decrease term size (R_mango_grape, R_peach_*, R_banana_zero, R_cherry_refl)
2. R_apple_orange appears problematic: `banana b s (grape n) → pear s (banana b s n)` 
   - Creates a pear wrapper while preserving banana
   - **But**: peels off one grape layer from banana's third argument  
   - **Crucially**: `pear` has no reduction rules, cannot create new bananas or grapes
3. R_cherry_diff may increase size but produces irreducible `mango (peach a b)` and cannot create new cherry constructors

**Why it terminates**: No rule can create grape or banana constructors in a way that enables infinite R_apple_orange chains. The "fuel" for R_apple_orange (grapes in banana third arguments) is bounded by the initial term and monotonically decreases.

## Proof Roadmap (within boundaries):

**Measure**: Lexicographic tuple (d₁, d₂, d₃):
- d₁ = Σ over all `banana b s t` subterms: number of consecutive leading `grape` constructors in `t`
- d₂ = count of `cherry` constructor nodes  
- d₃ = total term size

**Proof**: Show each rule application (extended with standard congruence closure) strictly decreases this measure:
- R_apple_orange: decreases d₁ (removes one grape from a banana's third arg)
- R_cherry_*: decreases d₂ or d₃
- Others: decrease d₃ (when d₁ and d₂ unchanged)

Well-foundedness of lexicographic ℕ³ gives SN. No external axioms needed beyond structural induction on the Trace inductive type.
