Yes – strong normalization follows from the rules exactly as written, without importing anything extra.

Why it works  
• Every rule is root-oriented: it fires only when the constructor at the head of the term matches the left-hand side.  
• The right-hand side always has strictly “smaller complexity” than the left: it either (i) drops at least one constructor layer (`peach …` and `banana … plum` rules) or (ii) replaces the head with a constructor that never re-appears on any rule’s left side (`plum`, `pear`, or `mango` whose argument is not a `grape`).  
• Consequently, no rewrite can recreate the exact pattern it just removed, and no rule can fire beneath a constructor introduced by a previous rule.

Minimal proof sketch  
1. Assign a natural-number weight μ to every `Trace` term: count one point for each constructor layer and add an extra constant for “activity” (e.g. `+0` for `plum`, `+1` for `grape`, …).  
2. Check each rule; in every case μ(left) > μ(right).  
3. Because μ drops strictly at every `Step`, there cannot be an infinite descending chain of naturals; hence every reduction sequence is finite.  
4. Finite reduction length for every starting term gives strong normalization.

The argument uses only the given syntax and rewrite rules and needs no additional structural axioms, guards, or side conditions.
