## Assessment

**Yes — the literal rewrite relation `Step` in that file is strongly normalizing.**

The key reason is that `Step` is only a **root-step** relation. There are **no context/congruence rules**, no substitution machinery, and no extra structural closure. So the only reductions available are the eight constructors of `Step` themselves.

## Why this is enough

Every rule does one of two things:

1. **Returns a proper subterm**, namely:
   - `R_peach_plum_left : peach plum t → t`
   - `R_peach_plum_right : peach t plum → t`
   - `R_peach_cancel : peach t t → t`
   - `R_banana_zero : banana b s plum → b`

   If reduction continues after one of these, it continues on a **strict subterm** of the previous term.

2. **Jumps to a root-normal form (for this calculus)**:
   - `R_mango_grape : mango (grape t) → plum`
   - `R_cherry_refl : cherry a a → plum`
   - `R_apple_orange : banana b s (grape n) → pear s (banana b s n)`
   - `R_cherry_diff : cherry a b → mango (peach a b)`

   These targets are root-irreducible under the given rules:
   - `plum` has no outgoing rule.
   - `pear ...` has no outgoing rule.
   - `mango (peach a b)` cannot match `R_mango_grape`, since `peach` and `grape` are distinct constructors of the inductive type `Trace`.

So any multi-step reduction can only keep going by repeatedly moving to a **proper subterm**. But proper-subterm descent on finite first-order terms is well-founded. Hence no infinite reduction sequence exists.

## Short proof roadmap

A clean proof stays entirely within the presented calculus:

- Define the **strict proper-subterm relation** on `Trace`.
- Observe:
  - if `u → v` and `v` is reducible again, then `v` must be one of the returned subterms `t` or `b`, hence `v` is a proper subterm of `u`;
  - otherwise `v` is already root-normal (`plum`, `pear ...`, or `mango (peach ...)`).
- Therefore any infinite `Step`-chain would induce an infinite descending chain of proper subterms of a finite term, impossible.
- Conclude strong normalization of `Step`.
