alone, **strong normalization can be established without adding any extra rewrite rules, guards, side conditions, structural axioms, or object-level assumptions**.

In fact, there are two readings:

- **If you mean the literal inductive relation `Step` exactly as written**, it is only a **root-step** relation, so termination is even easier.
- **If you mean the standard first-order rewrite relation generated by these rules under contexts and substitutions**, it is **still strongly normalizing**.

The only slight trap is that raw term size is not enough, because:
- `R_rec_succ` can increase size syntactically, and
- `R_eq_diff` also increases size by wrapping with `integrate (merge ...)`.

So a naive “size strictly decreases” proof does **not** work. But a standard **polynomial interpretation** does.

## Short proof roadmap

Interpret terms into $\mathbb{N}$ by a strictly monotone algebra, for example:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\llbracket \texttt{void} \rrbracket &= 0 \\
\llbracket \texttt{delta}(x) \rrbracket &= x + 1 \\
\llbracket \texttt{integrate}(x) \rrbracket &= x + 1 \\
\llbracket \texttt{merge}(x,y) \rrbracket &= x + y + 1 \\
\llbracket \texttt{app}(x,y) \rrbracket &= x + y \\
\llbracket \texttt{recΔ}(b,s,n) \rrbracket &= (n+1)(s+1) + b \\
\llbracket \texttt{eqW}(a,b) \rrbracket &= a + b + 3
\end{aligned}
$$

Then every rewrite rule strictly decreases this measure:

- `integrate (delta t) → void`:
  $$
  \llbracket \texttt{integrate(delta t)} \rrbracket = \llbracket t \rrbracket + 2 > 0
  $$
- `merge void t → t`, `merge t void → t`, `merge t t → t`:
  all immediate from $x+y+1$.
- `recΔ b s void → b`:
  $$
  (0+1)(\llbracket s \rrbracket+1)+\llbracket b \rrbracket > \llbracket b \rrbracket
  $$
- `recΔ b s (delta n) → app s (recΔ b s n)`:
  $$
  (\llbracket n \rrbracket+2)(\llbracket s \rrbracket+1)+\llbracket b \rrbracket
  >
  \llbracket s \rrbracket + (\llbracket n \rrbracket+1)(\llbracket s \rrbracket+1)+\llbracket b \rrbracket
  $$
  and the difference is exactly $1$.
- `eqW a a → void`:
  $$
  2\llbracket a \rrbracket + 3 > 0
  $$
- `eqW a b → integrate (merge a b)`:
  $$
  \llbracket \texttt{eqW}(a,b) \rrbracket
  =
  \llbracket a \rrbracket + \llbracket b \rrbracket + 3
  >
  \llbracket a \rrbracket + \llbracket b \rrbracket + 2
  =
  \llbracket \texttt{integrate(merge(a,b))} \rrbracket
  $$

Because the interpretation is into the well-founded order $>$ on $\mathbb{N}$ and all symbols are monotone, this gives a standard termination proof for the rewrite system.

## Bottom line

So the raw answer is:

- **Yes, strong normalization is provable from the presented calculus alone.**
- **No extra structural axioms or hidden semantic assumptions are needed.**
- The right proof is **not** a plain size argument, but a **polynomial interpretation / reduction ordering** argument.
