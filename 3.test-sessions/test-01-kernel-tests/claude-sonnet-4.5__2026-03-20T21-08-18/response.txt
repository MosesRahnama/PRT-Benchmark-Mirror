1. **No congruence rules specified**: The `Step` relation only defines head reduction. A complete rewrite system needs congruence rules to reduce under constructors. Without these, the system is incomplete; with them, you need to prove termination of the closure.

   - `R_rec_succ` strictly decreases delta-depth in the third argument
   - No rule can regenerate `delta` constructors in positions that would restart recursion

### Could it be proven?

**Maybe**, but you'd need:
- A **lexicographic ordering** on (delta-depth, other metrics)
- Or a **polynomial interpretation** assigning weights to constructors
- These require meta-theoretic frameworks (well-founded induction, ordinal assignments) which are arguably "structural axioms"

You asked if it can be proven "without importing structural axioms or object-level assumptions beyond what is derivable from the given syntax and rewrite rules." The syntax and rules alone don't prove termination — you need a *meta-theorem* about well-foundedness that lives outside the calculus. That's unavoidable for any termination proof.

**Bottom line**: The system likely terminates, but proving it requires proof machinery external to the calculus itself.
