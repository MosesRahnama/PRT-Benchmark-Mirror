**Raw assessment:** **Yes.** Strong normalization can be established from
alone, with no extra rewrite rules, guards, or object-level axioms.

A direct proof works by a **polynomial interpretation** into $\mathbb N$ that strictly decreases on every rule. For example:

$$
\begin{aligned}
|void|&=0 \\
|\delta(t)|&=|t|+1 \\
|integrate(t)|&=|t|+1 \\
|merge(x,y)|&=|x|+|y|+1 \\
|app(x,y)|&=|x|+|y| \\
|rec\Delta(b,s,n)|&=(|n|+1)(|s|+1)+|b| \\
|eqW(a,b)|&=|a|+|b|+3
\end{aligned}
$$

Then each rewrite decreases strictly, e.g.

- $|integrate(\delta t)| = |t|+2 > 0 = |void|$
- $|merge(void,t)| = |t|+1 > |t|$
- $|merge(t,t)| = 2|t|+1 > |t|$
- $|rec\Delta(b,s,void)| = |s|+|b|+1 > |b|$
- $|rec\Delta(b,s,\delta n)| = (|n|+2)(|s|+1)+|b| > |s| + (|n|+1)(|s|+1)+|b| = |app(s,rec\Delta(b,s,n))|$
- $|eqW(a,b)| = |a|+|b|+3 > |a|+|b|+2 = |integrate(merge(a,b))|$

Because the interpretation is monotone in every argument, the decrease is stable under standard first-order rewriting in contexts. So no infinite reduction sequence exists.

**Short roadmap:** prove termination by this interpretation, then conclude strong normalization for all finite `Trace` terms. The overlap at `eqW a a` is not an obstacle for normalization; both possible rewrites still decrease.
