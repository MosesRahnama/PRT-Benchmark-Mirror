**Yes — strong normalization is derivable from the presented calculus alone**, without additional axioms, guards, or structural imports. The standard proof method is Lexicographic Path Ordering (LPO).

## Termination Order

**Precedence:**
$$\mathtt{eqW} \succ \mathtt{rec\Delta} \succ \mathtt{app} \succ \mathtt{integrate} \succ \mathtt{merge} \succ \mathtt{delta} \succ \mathtt{void}$$

Under LPO with this precedence, every rule strictly decreases the term measure.

## Proof Roadmap (rules checked in pairs by difficulty)

**Rules 1–5, 7 — trivial (subterm or top-symbol drop):**

| Rule | Argument |
|---|---|
| R_int_delta | `integrate > void`; unique arg `delta(t)` is gone → head drops |
| R_merge_void_left/right | RHS is a proper subterm (case 1 of LPO) |
| R_merge_cancel | RHS `t` is a proper subterm of `merge(t,t)` (case 1) |
| R_rec_zero | RHS `b` is a proper subterm of `recΔ(b,s,void)` |
| R_eq_refl | `eqW > void`; constant RHS, head drops |

**Rule 6 — R_rec_succ (the only size-increasing rule, requires case 3):**

$$\mathtt{rec\Delta}(b,s,\mathtt{delta}(n)) \succ_{\mathrm{lpo}} \mathtt{app}(s,\,\mathtt{rec\Delta}(b,s,n))$$

- $\mathtt{rec\Delta} \succ \mathtt{app}$, so case 2 applies; need both args of RHS $\prec$ LHS:
  - $s$ is a direct subterm of LHS ✓
  - $\mathtt{rec\Delta}(b,s,n) \prec_{\mathrm{lpo}} \mathtt{rec\Delta}(b,s,\mathtt{delta}(n))$: same head, lex comparison reaches the third argument, where $n \prec_{\mathrm{lpo}} \mathtt{delta}(n)$ by the subterm property ✓

**Rule 8 — R_eq_diff (also size-increasing, requires case 2 twice):**

$$\mathtt{eqW}(a,b) \succ_{\mathrm{lpo}} \mathtt{integrate}(\mathtt{merge}(a,b))$$

- $\mathtt{eqW} \succ \mathtt{integrate}$; need the sole arg $\mathtt{merge}(a,b) \prec_{\mathrm{lpo}} \mathtt{eqW}(a,b)$:
  - $\mathtt{eqW} \succ \mathtt{merge}$; args of $\mathtt{merge}(a,b)$ are $a,b$, both proper subterms of LHS ✓

## Subtleties That Raise No Obstruction

**Non-linear patterns** (R_merge_cancel `merge(t,t)→t`, R_eq_refl `eqW(a,a)→void`): LPO-orientation relies only on the subterm property of LHS; non-linearity in the LHS pattern is not a problem because the subterm argument holds regardless of whether variables coincide.

**Overlap of R_eq_refl and R_eq_diff**: Both rules apply to `eqW(t,t)`. For SN we need every single step to decrease the LPO measure — both do, independently. Confluence is a separate question and not required here.

**No rule produces `eqW`**: the `eqW` constructor is monotone-decreasing over any reduction, providing an additional witness that the system cannot cycle through `eqW`-headed terms.
