**No**, strong normalization cannot be established from the presented calculus alone.

The given first-order rewrite system supplies only a syntax (ground terms with no binders) and a fixed set of object-level rewrite rules. Any proof of SN requires exhibiting a well-founded strict order (or measure) on terms such that every rule application produces a strictly smaller term. No such order, ranking function, or induction principle is derivable purely from the syntax and the rules shown; establishing well-foundedness inevitably imports meta-theoretic notions (ordinal measures, syntactic size, multiset orders, etc.) that lie outside the calculus. Standard termination techniques are therefore inapplicable without adding structural axioms, extra rules, or hidden side conditions, which are explicitly disallowed. The system as stated is therefore insufficient.
