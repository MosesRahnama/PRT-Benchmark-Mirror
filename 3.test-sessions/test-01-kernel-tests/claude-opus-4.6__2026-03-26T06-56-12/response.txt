**Yes.** Strong normalization of this system — under the compatible (contextual) closure of `Step` — is provable purely from the presented syntax and rules, using a standard polynomial interpretation over $\mathbb{N}$. No extra axioms, guards, or side conditions are needed.

## Proof Roadmap

### The system at a glance

- **Ground TRS**: `Trace` is an inductive type with no variables. All terms are finite. `app` is an inert binary constructor (no rule reduces it).
- **8 root-rewrite rules** define `Step`. The only non-trivial features are:
  - **Rule 6** (`recΔ` unfolding) duplicates the subterm `s`.
  - **Rules 7 & 8** (`eqW`) overlap when $a = b$, introducing non-determinism.

### Method: strictly monotone weight function

Define $w : \text{Trace} \to \mathbb{N}^{+}$:

$$
\begin{aligned}
w(\texttt{void}) &= 1 \\
w(\texttt{delta}\; t) &= w(t) + 1 \\
w(\texttt{integrate}\; t) &= w(t) + 1 \\
w(\texttt{merge}\; t_1\; t_2) &= w(t_1) + w(t_2) + 1 \\
w(\texttt{app}\; t_1\; t_2) &= w(t_1) + w(t_2) + 1 \\
w(\texttt{recΔ}\; b\; s\; n) &= (w(s)+2)\cdot w(n) + w(b) + 1 \\
w(\texttt{eqW}\; a\; b) &= w(a) + w(b) + 3
\end{aligned}
$$

### Verification (each rule strictly decreases $w$)

| Rule | LHS weight | RHS weight | Difference |
|---|---|---|---|
| R\_int\_delta | $w(t)+2$ | $1$ | $w(t)+1 > 0$ |
| R\_merge\_void\_left | $w(t)+2$ | $w(t)$ | $2 > 0$ |
| R\_merge\_void\_right | $w(t)+2$ | $w(t)$ | $2 > 0$ |
| R\_merge\_cancel | $2w(t)+1$ | $w(t)$ | $w(t)+1 > 0$ |
| R\_rec\_zero | $w(s)+w(b)+3$ | $w(b)$ | $w(s)+3 > 0$ |
| R\_rec\_succ | $(w(s){+}2)(w(n){+}1)+w(b)+1$ | $w(s)+(w(s){+}2)w(n)+w(b)+2$ | $\mathbf{1}$ |
| R\_eq\_refl | $2w(a)+3$ | $1$ | $2w(a)+2 > 0$ |
| R\_eq\_diff | $w(a)+w(b)+3$ | $w(a)+w(b)+2$ | $\mathbf{1}$ |

The critical rule is **R\_rec\_succ** (Rule 6). Expanding:

$$
\text{LHS} = (w(s)+2)\,w(n) + w(s) + 2 + w(b) + 1
$$
$$
\text{RHS} = w(s) + (w(s)+2)\,w(n) + w(b) + 1 + 1
$$
$$
\text{LHS} - \text{RHS} = (w(s)+2+w(b)+1) - (w(s)+w(b)+2) = 1
$$

The multiplicative component $(w(s)+2)\cdot w(n)$ in the `recΔ` weight absorbs the duplication of `s` in the right-hand side of Rule 6 — this is exactly why a simple additive weight would fail but a polynomial interpretation succeeds.

### Monotonicity (extension to contextual closure)

$w$ is **strictly monotone** in every argument position:

- All additive positions (`delta □`, `integrate □`, `merge □ t`, `app □ t`, etc.): trivially monotone.
- `recΔ □ s n` ($b$-position): additive in $w(b)$. ✓
- `recΔ b □ n` ($s$-position): coefficient is $w(n) \geq 1$, so $(w(s)+2)\cdot w(n)$ is strictly monotone in $w(s)$. ✓
- `recΔ b s □` ($n$-position): coefficient is $w(s)+2 \geq 3$, so strictly monotone in $w(n)$. ✓

Therefore, if a root step decreases $w$, the same step applied in any one-hole context also decreases $w$.

### Conclusion

Every reduction step (in any context, choosing any applicable rule non-deterministically) strictly decreases $w \in \mathbb{N}^{+}$. Since $\mathbb{N}$ is well-founded, all reduction sequences are finite. **Strong normalization holds.**

The overlap between R\_eq\_refl and R\_eq\_diff when $a = b$ is harmless: both branches strictly decrease $w$, so every non-deterministic choice terminates. No disambiguation guard or priority is needed.
