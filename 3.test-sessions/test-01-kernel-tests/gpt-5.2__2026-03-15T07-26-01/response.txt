## Why termination looks provable *internally* (no extra axioms)

Key structural facts derivable just from the signature + rules:

- **No rule introduces `eqW`** on the right-hand side. So the number of `eqW` nodes strictly decreases whenever an `eqW` redex is contracted, and can never go back up.
- **No rule introduces `recΔ`** except the `recΔ` already present in `R_rec_succ`’s RHS (it preserves the count of `recΔ` occurrences: 1 stays 1; `R_rec_zero` decreases it).
- **No rule introduces a new outer `delta`** (indeed, no RHS has `delta` at the root). So the “available fuel” in the third argument of any `recΔ` is bounded by the number of leading `delta` constructors already present in that subterm.
- `merge`/`integrate` rules are all **collapsing** (they strictly simplify structure: remove `merge`, remove `integrate∘delta`, etc.). `R_eq_diff` is the only rule that can increase raw symbol count locally, but it strictly eliminates an `eqW` and does not create any symbol that can recreate `eqW`, so it cannot sustain an infinite chain.

## A short proof roadmap that stays within your boundary

A clean “standard method” route (no extra rewrite rules, no side conditions):

1. **Define the full rewrite relation** as the compatible/contextual closure of `Step` (if that’s your intended operational semantics). This is definitional, not an added axiom/rule.
2. Prove termination by orienting all eight rules with a **simplification order**, e.g. **Recursive Path Ordering (RPO)**:
   - Choose a precedence such as  
     \[
     \texttt{recΔ} \;>\; \texttt{app} \;>\; \texttt{eqW} \;>\; \texttt{integrate} \;>\; \texttt{merge} \;>\; \texttt{delta} \;>\; \texttt{void}.
     \]
   - Give `recΔ` a **lexicographic status that compares the 3rd argument first**, so that  
     `recΔ b s (delta n)  >  recΔ b s n` holds because `delta n > n` (subterm property).
   - Then check each rule:
     - Collapsing rules (`merge` void/cancel, `integrate(delta _)`, `recΔ _ _ void`, `eqW a a`) are oriented because a compound term is greater than its proper subterms/constants in RPO.
     - `R_eq_diff`: `eqW a b > integrate(merge a b)` holds because `eqW > integrate` and `eqW a b` dominates the argument `merge a b` (again by subterm dominance + precedence).
     - `R_rec_succ`: `recΔ b s (delta n) > app s (recΔ b s n)` holds because `recΔ > app` and (crucially) `recΔ b s (delta n) > recΔ b s n` by the “3rd argument first” status.
3. Use the standard theorem: **RPO is well-founded on finite first-order terms**, hence no infinite descending chains, hence the rewrite relation is terminating ⇒ **strong normalization**.

This stays entirely at the meta-level (induction/well-foundedness on finite syntax trees) and doesn’t smuggle in any object theory or additional rewriting power.
