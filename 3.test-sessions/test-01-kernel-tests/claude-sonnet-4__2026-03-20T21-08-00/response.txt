## Brief Analysis

The system presents a first-order rewrite calculus with 7 constructors and 8 rewrite rules. Most rules are clearly size-reducing:

- `integrate (delta t) → void` (eliminates structure)
- `merge` rules establish void as identity and merge as idempotent

- `eqW a a → void` (reflexivity reduces to void)

The potentially problematic rules are:

- `R_eq_diff`: `eqW a b → integrate (merge a b)` - can temporarily increase term size

2. **Prove termination** by showing each rule decreases this ordering:

   - Other rules decrease secondary measure  
   - The `app` introduction in `R_rec_succ` is bounded by the recursion structure

3. **Key insight**: The `recΔ` recursion provides a "fuel" mechanism that bounds the depth of computation chains, while other rules provide cleanup operations that eventually reach normal forms.

The system appears well-designed for strong normalization without additional axioms, as the structural recursion in `recΔ` provides the necessary decreasing metric.
