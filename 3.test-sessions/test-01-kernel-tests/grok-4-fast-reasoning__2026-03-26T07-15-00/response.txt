### Short Proof Roadmap
Define the **delta-height** $h(t)$ recursively: $h(\mathsf{void}) = 0$, $h(\delta\, t) = h(t) + 1$, and for other constructors $c\, \bar{u}$, $h(c\, \bar{u}) = \max_i h(u_i)$.

Define the termination measure on traces $t$ as the lexicographic triple $(M_1(t), M_2(t), M_3(t))$:
- $M_1(t)$: Multiset of $h(n)$ over all subterms $\mathsf{rec}\Delta\, b\, s\, n$ in $t$, ordered by the multiset extension of $\geq$ on $\mathbb{N}$ (larger heights dominate).
- $M_2(t)$: Total number of $\mathsf{eqW}$ subterms in $t$.
- $M_3(t)$: Structural size $|t|$ (number of constructor applications, e.g., $|\mathsf{void}| = 0$, $|c\, \bar{u}| = 1 + \sum |u_i|$).

This measure is well-founded ($\mathbb{N}^\infty$-multisets $\times \mathbb{N} \times \mathbb{N}$ under lex order). Verify each rule decreases it (contextual closure follows by subterm monotonicity):

- **R_int_delta**: $M_1$ unchanged or decreases (subterm heights preserved/max drops if $\delta\, t$ held recs); $M_2$ unchanged; $| \mathsf{integrate}\, (\delta\, t) | = |t| + 2 > 0 = |\mathsf{void}|$, so $M_3$ decreases.
- **R_merge_void_left/right**: $M_1, M_2$ unchanged (void adds nothing); size drops by 1 (removes merge/void).
- **R_merge_cancel**: $M_1$ decreases (duplicated heights in $t$ halved); $M_2$ decreases (eqWs halved); size drops by $|t| + 1$.
- **R_rec_zero**: $M_1$ decreases (removes height-0 entry); $M_2$ drops by eqWs in $s$; size drops by $|s| + 1$.
- **R_rec_succ**: Let height $h = h(n) + 1$. $M_1$ decreases (replaces $h$ with $h(n) < h$, others unchanged); $M_2$ may increase (duplicates eqWs in $s$), but primary $M_1$ dominates; size may increase, irrelevant.
- **R_eq_refl**: $M_1$ unchanged; $M_2$ drops by 1 + eqWs in $a$; size drops to 0.
- **R_eq_diff**: $M_1$ unchanged; $M_2$ drops by 1; size increases by 1, but $M_2$ dominates.

Reductions inside subterms decrease the measure by induction on subterm size. Thus, no infinite sequences exist, proving strong normalization.
