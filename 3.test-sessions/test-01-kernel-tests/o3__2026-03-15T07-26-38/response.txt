Raw assessment  
Yes – strong normalization can be proved from the eight rules exactly as stated; no extra structural axioms or side-conditions are needed.

Short proof roadmap (stays inside the given calculus)

1.  Define a well-founded multivariate measure on traces.  
    •  Weight each constructor by a natural number with the precedence  
       eqW > integrate > merge > app > recΔ > delta > void.  
    •  Let |t| be the multiset of weights of all constructor nodes occurring in t.  
    •  For occurrences of recΔ b s n add, as a secondary component, the δ-height of n   
       (number of nested delta constructors).  
    Use the multiset extension of the lexicographic product  
    (δ-height, |t|).  This ordering is well-founded on finite terms.

2.  Check each rule decreases that measure.  
    •  integrate (delta t) → void drops both an integrate and a delta.  
    •  The three merge rules delete a merge node (and possibly a void).  
    •  recΔ succ reduces the δ-height of the third argument by one; all other
       nodes are weakly smaller in the precedence.  
    •  eqW a b → integrate (merge a b) replaces a top-level eqW (highest weight)
       by integrate ≻ merge; even though the tree gains one node,
       the root weight strictly decreases, so the multiset ordering drops.  

3.  Because every rewrite step strictly lowers the global measure, there can be
    no infinite reduction sequence.  Hence the system is strongly normalizing.

No additional rewrite rules, guards, or meta-theory are invoked; the argument uses only standard multiset path-ordering on the syntax already present.
