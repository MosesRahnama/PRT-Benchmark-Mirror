$$
\texttt{cherry} \succ \texttt{mango} \succ \texttt{peach}, \qquad
\texttt{banana} \succ \texttt{pear}, \qquad
\texttt{grape} \succ \texttt{plum}.
$$

Then each rule decreases:

- `peach plum t → t`, `peach t plum → t`, `peach t t → t`, `banana b s plum → b`

- `mango (grape t) → plum` and `cherry a a → plum`  
  decrease to a smaller constant term.
- `cherry a b → mango (peach a b)`  
  decreases because the root symbol drops from `cherry` to `mango`, and the left side dominates the arguments by subterm property.
