## Short proof roadmap

A simple lexicographic measure on terms suffices. For example, define a triple $(E, D, S)$ where:

- $E(t)$ = number of `eqW` nodes in $t$  
- $D(t)$ = sum of “delta‑depths” of the third argument of each `recΔ` (i.e., how many leading `delta` constructors it has; 0 otherwise)  
- $S(t)$ = total constructor size of $t$

Order these lexicographically: $(E,D,S)$ with the usual $<$ on $\mathbb{N}$.

Then check each rule:

- `R_eq_refl` and `R_eq_diff` strictly decrease $E$ (no rule introduces `eqW`).
- `R_rec_succ` keeps $E$ fixed but strictly decreases $D$ (the `delta` in the third argument is peeled).
- `R_rec_zero`, `R_int_delta`, and all `merge` rules strictly decrease $S$ without increasing $E$ or $D$.

Thus every step strictly decreases the lexicographic measure, which is well‑founded. Hence there are no infinite rewrite sequences (strong normalization).
