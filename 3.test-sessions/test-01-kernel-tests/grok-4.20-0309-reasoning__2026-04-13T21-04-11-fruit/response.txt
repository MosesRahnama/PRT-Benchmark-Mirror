**Yes, strong normalization can be established from the presented calculus alone.**

The `Step` relation is an inductively-defined, root-only reduction (no congruence/context closure is derivable from the given constructors). Every rule either:
- collapses a redex to a strictly smaller proper subterm (`peach` rules, `banana_zero`, `cherry_refl`), or
- replaces a specific top-level pattern with a term whose head constructor (`plum`, `pear`, or a `mango`/`peach` combination) matches no LHS of any rule.

No rule introduces a new root redex whose further reduction can recreate a prior pattern in a way that permits an infinite chain. The `banana` recursion on `grape` peels one constructor from the third argument but yields a `pear`-headed term from which no further root `Step` is possible. The `cherry_diff` case (under the derivable `a ≠ b` distinction on the inductive type `Trace`) yields `mango (peach a b)`, which is a redex only in the trivial `mango (grape _)` case already covered by an existing rule that collapses to `plum`.

A straightforward structural induction on `Trace` (using only the inductive syntax and the given rules) shows every term has finite depth of applicable root redexes; the induction hypothesis applies directly to the RHS subterms produced by the `peach`/`banana_zero`/`cherry` rules. No well-founded order external to the syntax, no extra rewrite rules, no guards, and no object-level assumptions beyond what the inductive definitions themselves provide are required. Hence SN follows directly.
