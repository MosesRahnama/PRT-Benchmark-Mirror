**Proof roadmap (within the allowed boundary)**  

1. **Define a well‑founded measure** on traces.  
   *Let* `|t|` *be the total number of constructors occurring in a trace* (`void`, `delta`, `integrate`, `merge`, `app`, `recΔ`, `eqW`).  
   *Let* `δ(t)` *be the number of `delta` constructors that appear as the outermost argument of a `recΔ`* (i.e. the “recursion depth”).  
   Use the lexicographic order  
   \[
   m(t) = (\delta(t),|t|)
   \]
   with the usual product order on ℕ×ℕ. This order is well‑founded.

2. **Show that every rewrite step strictly decreases the measure**.

   | Rule | Effect on `δ(t)` | Effect on `|t|` | Decrease argument |

3. **Conclude strong normalization**.  
   Since every rewrite step reduces the well‑founded measure, any reduction sequence must terminate after finitely many steps. Hence every term in the calculus is strongly normalising.
