**Raw assessment:** **Yes.** Strong normalization can be established from
alone, without adding any extra rewrite rules, guards, side conditions, or object-level axioms.

Two points:

1. **Literal Lean reading:** `Step` is only a **root-step** relation; no context-closure rule is defined. On that reading, normalization is already very easy.
2. **Even under the usual TRS reading** (closure under contexts/substitutions), the system is still terminating by a standard **polynomial interpretation** over $\mathbb N$.

### Short proof roadmap
Assign a natural-number measure $|t|$ by:
$$
|\texttt{void}| = 0
$$
$$
|\texttt{delta}\ t| = |t| + 1,\qquad
|\texttt{integrate}\ t| = |t|
$$
$$
|\texttt{merge}\ a\ b| = |a| + |b| + 1,\qquad
|\texttt{app}\ a\ b| = |a| + |b|
$$
$$
|\texttt{eqW}\ a\ b| = |a| + |b| + 2
$$
$$
|\texttt{recΔ}\ b\ s\ n| = (|n|+1)(|s|+1) + |b|
$$

Then every rule strictly decreases this measure:

- `integrate (delta t) → void`:
  $|t|+1 > 0$
- `merge void t → t`, `merge t void → t`, `merge t t → t`:
  $0+|t|+1 > |t|$, $|t|+0+1 > |t|$, $2|t|+1 > |t|$
- `recΔ b s void → b`:
  $(0+1)(|s|+1)+|b| > |b|$
- `recΔ b s (delta n) → app s (recΔ b s n)`:
  $$
  (|n|+2)(|s|+1)+|b|
  >
  |s| + (|n|+1)(|s|+1) + |b|
  $$
  with difference exactly $1$
- `eqW a a → void`:
  $2|a|+2 > 0$
- `eqW a b → integrate (merge a b)`:
  $|a|+|b|+2 > |a|+|b|+1$

So each rewrite step strictly decreases a well-founded measure on $\mathbb N$. Hence there is **no infinite reduction sequence**.

### Bottom line
The calculus as presented is sufficient for strong normalization. The only caveat is terminological: if one reads `Step` **literally**, the result is even more immediate because no contextual reduction rule is present. But even under the standard rewrite-calculus interpretation, the system remains strongly normalizing.
