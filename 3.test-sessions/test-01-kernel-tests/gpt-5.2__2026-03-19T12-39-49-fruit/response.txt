## Why termination is still provable (in the standard TRS sense)

A standard, assumption-free route is to use a **simplification order** such as **Lexicographic Path Ordering (LPO)** (or a closely related recursive path ordering). These orders are:

- defined purely from the **signature** (your constructors) plus a **precedence** on symbols,
- **well-founded** on finite first-order terms,
- and can orient rules even when variables are duplicated.

### Sketchy-but-sufficient roadmap (stays within your boundary)

1. **Define the rewrite relation** you care about:
   - either the given `Step`, or
   - its usual **contextual closure** (rewrite in any subterm).

2. **Choose an LPO precedence** that makes every LHS strictly greater than its RHS. One workable precedence is:
   \[
   \texttt{banana} \;>\; \texttt{pear} \;>\; \texttt{cherry} \;>\; \texttt{mango} \;>\; \texttt{peach} \;>\; \texttt{grape} \;>\; \texttt{plum}.
   \]
   (Exact middle ordering isn’t unique; key constraints are `banana > pear` and `cherry > mango`/`cherry > peach`.)

3. **Check each rule decreases under LPO**:
   - Rules like `peach plum t → t`, `peach t t → t`, `banana b s plum → b` decrease by the **subterm property** (a term is bigger than its proper subterms).
   - `mango (grape t) → plum` decreases because `mango > plum` in the precedence.
   - The “hard” rule `banana b s (grape n) → pear s (banana b s n)` is handled because:
     - `banana > pear`, so it suffices (in LPO) to show the LHS is bigger than both arguments of the RHS (`s` and `banana b s n`);
     - `s` is a subterm of the LHS, so LHS > `s`;
     - and `banana b s (grape n) > banana b s n` holds because `grape n > n` (subterm property) and the `banana` arguments are compared lexicographically (or via the standard LPO same-root comparison).

4. Conclude: since LPO is **well-founded**, there is **no infinite descending chain**, hence **no infinite rewrite sequence** ⇒ strong normalization.

### About the conditional rule (`R_cherry_diff`)

`R_cherry_diff` requires a proof of `a ≠ b` to fire. For termination, this condition only *reduces* the set of possible steps; it can’t create divergence. Even if you pretended it were unconditional, its orientation under LPO (with `cherry` high in the precedence) still goes through.
