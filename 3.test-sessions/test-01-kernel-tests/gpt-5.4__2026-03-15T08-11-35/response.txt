## Why this works

Interpret each constructor by a natural-number valued function:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\llbracket \text{void} \rrbracket &:= 0 \\
\llbracket \delta(t) \rrbracket &:= \llbracket t \rrbracket + 1 \\
\llbracket \text{integrate}(t) \rrbracket &:= \llbracket t \rrbracket + 1 \\
\llbracket \text{merge}(a,b) \rrbracket &:= \llbracket a \rrbracket + \llbracket b \rrbracket + 1 \\
\llbracket \text{app}(s,x) \rrbracket &:= \llbracket x \rrbracket \\
\llbracket \text{rec}\Delta(b,s,n) \rrbracket &:= \llbracket b \rrbracket + \llbracket n \rrbracket + 1 \\
\llbracket \text{eqW}(a,b) \rrbracket &:= \llbracket a \rrbracket + \llbracket b \rrbracket + 3
\end{aligned}
$$

All these interpretations are weakly monotone, and now each rule strictly decreases:

- $\text{integrate}(\delta t) \to \text{void}$

  $$
  \llbracket \text{integrate}(\delta t) \rrbracket
  = \llbracket t \rrbracket + 2
  > 0
  = \llbracket \text{void} \rrbracket
  $$

- $\text{merge void } t \to t$

  $$
  0 + \llbracket t \rrbracket + 1 > \llbracket t \rrbracket
  $$

- $\text{merge } t \ \text{void} \to t$

  same argument.

- $\text{merge } t \ t \to t$

  $$
  2\llbracket t \rrbracket + 1 > \llbracket t \rrbracket

- $\text{rec}\Delta(b,s,\text{void}) \to b$

  \llbracket b \rrbracket + (\llbracket n \rrbracket + 1) + 1

  $$
  \llbracket \text{app}(s,\text{rec}\Delta(b,s,n)) \rrbracket

  so it decreases strictly.

Since every rewrite step strictly lowers a natural-number measure, **there cannot be an infinite reduction sequence**.
