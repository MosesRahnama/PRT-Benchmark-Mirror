None of the three proposed termination measures (mu1, mu2, or mu3) is valid for proving termination of the rewrite system. For each measure, there exist rewrite steps (specifically, applications of the R_rec_succ rule) where the measure does not strictly decrease—in fact, it either stays the same (when the measure of the step argument `s` is 0) or increases (when the measure of `s` is greater than 0). A valid termination measure must strictly decrease on every possible rewrite step to ensure no infinite rewrite chains are possible.
