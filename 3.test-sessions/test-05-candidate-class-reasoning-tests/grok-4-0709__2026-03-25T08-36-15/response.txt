- **Behavior on Other Rules**: All measures strictly decrease (`LHS > RHS`) for R1–R5 and R7–R8, as their constants ensure a net positive drop (e.g., removing constructors or simplifying merges/equalities).
- **Implications for Termination**:
  - None of the measures can prove termination because they do not strictly decrease across *all* rules. The system may still terminate (e.g., recursion unfolds finitely many times before base-casing on R5), but these measures cannot demonstrate it via a simple strictly decreasing Nat-valued function.
  - A more advanced proof technique (e.g., dependency pairs, lexicographic orders, or multiset orders) might be needed to handle the temporary increase during unfolding.
