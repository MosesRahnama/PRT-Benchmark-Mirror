## Assessment of the Three Candidate Termination Measures

### Setup

The file defines a first-order rewrite calculus over the `Trace` type with 8 rewrite rules (`Step`), and three candidate natural-number-valued measures `mu1`, `mu2`, `mu3`. A valid termination measure must satisfy $\mu(\text{lhs}) > \mu(\text{rhs})$ for every rule instance.

---

### Rule-by-rule verdicts

I'll abbreviate $\mu(t)$ as just $t$ in the arithmetic below. All seven "easy" rules are handled identically by all three candidates. The critical rule is **R_rec_succ**.

| Rule | LHS Ôćĺ RHS | mu1 margin | mu2 margin | mu3 margin |
|------|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| R_int_delta | `integrate (delta t) Ôćĺ void` | $2+t > 0$ Ôťô | $3+t > 0$ Ôťô | $3+t > 0$ Ôťô |
| R_merge_void_left | `merge void t Ôćĺ t` | $1$ Ôťô | $2$ Ôťô | $1$ Ôťô |
| R_merge_void_right | `merge t void Ôćĺ t` | $1$ Ôťô | $2$ Ôťô | $1$ Ôťô |

### Detailed analysis of the critical failure (R_rec_succ)

The rule `recDelta b s (delta n) Ôćĺ app s (recDelta b s n)` **duplicates** the sub-term `s`: the RHS contains `s` both as the first argument of `app` and inside the recursive `recDelta`. For any additive measure with constants $c_r, c_a, c_d$ for `recDelta`, `app`, `delta`:

$$\text{LHS} = c_r + \mu(b) + \mu(s) + c_d + \mu(n)$$
$$\text{RHS} = c_a + \mu(s) + c_r + \mu(b) + \mu(s) + \mu(n) = c_a + c_r + \mu(b) + 2\mu(s) + \mu(n)$$

The required inequality $\text{LHS} > \text{RHS}$ simplifies to:

$$c_d > c_a + \mu(s)$$

Since $\mu(s)$ is unbounded, **no choice of finite constants can satisfy this**. The deficit for each candidate:

| Candidate | Required (impossible) condition | Deficit when $s = \texttt{void}$ |
|-----------|-------------------------------|--------------------------------|
| **mu1** | $0 > \mu_1(s)$ | $0$ (non-strict: $0 \not> 0$) |
| **mu2** | $0 > \mu_2(s)$ | $0$ (non-strict: $0 \not> 0$) |
| **mu3** | $0 > 1 + \mu_3(s)$ | $1$ (always $\geq 1$) |

---

### Comparative ranking

1. **mu2 ÔÇö best of the three (but still unsound).** It passes 7 of 8 rules with the largest margins (e.g., margin 5 on R_rec_zero vs. 1 for the others), giving the most "room" on the rules it does handle. On R_rec_succ its deficit equals $\mu_2(s)$, same structural gap as mu1.

2. **mu1 ÔÇö tied-second.** Same deficit structure as mu2 on the failing rule ($\mu_1(s)$), but smaller margins on the passing rules (e.g., only 1 on R_merge and R_rec_zero).
