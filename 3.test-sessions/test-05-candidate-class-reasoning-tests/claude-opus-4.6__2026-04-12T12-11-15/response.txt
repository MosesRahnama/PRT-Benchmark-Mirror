### Verdict

| Candidate | Sound? | Failing rule(s) |
|---|---|---|
| **mu1** | **No** | R_rec_succ |
| **mu2** | **No** | R_rec_succ |
| **mu3** | **No** | R_rec_succ |

**None of the three candidates is a valid termination measure.** They all fail on `R_rec_succ` for the same structural reason: the rule `recDelta b s (delta n) вЖЭ app s (recDelta b s n)` duplicates the subterm `s` in the right-hand side. Any measure of the form $\mu(t) = c_t + \sum \mu(\text{subterms})$ (i.e., an additive/polynomial interpretation where subterms contribute linearly) cannot strictly decrease across a rule that copies a universally-quantified subterm. To handle this rule, one would need either:

1. **A non-additive measure** вАФ e.g., a multiset path ordering, a lexicographic ordering on a tuple (such as the depth of `n` as the primary component), or a Knuth-Bendix style polynomial where `s` is counted with coefficient < 1 in the recursive call context.
2. **A different proof strategy** вАФ e.g., proving termination via a well-founded relation on the third argument `n` alone (since `delta n вЖТ n` strictly shrinks it), treating `b` and `s` as parameters rather than measured arguments.
