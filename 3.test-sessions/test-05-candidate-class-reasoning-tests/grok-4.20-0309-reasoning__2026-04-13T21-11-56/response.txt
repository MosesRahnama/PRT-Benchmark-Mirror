**Assessment of the three termination measure candidates (mu1, mu2, mu3):**

All three candidates are **invalid** as strict termination measures (ranking functions) for the given rewrite relation `Step`.

### Summary of the rewrite rules
The `Step` relation encodes these one-step rewrites on `Trace`:
- `integrate (delta t) â†’ void`
- `merge void t â†’ t`, `merge t void â†’ t`, `merge t t â†’ t`

A valid measure `mu : Trace â†’ Nat` must satisfy **strict decrease** (`mu t > mu u`) for every `Step t u`.

### Why the failure occurs
The `R_rec_succ` rule "unfolds" a recursive trace while duplicating the step function `s` in the `app` constructor. All three measures count the duplicated `s` fully on the right-hand side (via both the `app` and inner `recDelta` cases) while only counting it once on the left, plus the structural cost of `app` vs. the `delta` on the argument. This produces non-decreasing (or increasing) values.

The other rules are unproblematic for all three measures (they produce strict decreases of at least 1).

### Conclusion
None of mu1, mu2, or mu3 can be used (alone or directly) to prove well-foundedness/termination of `Step`, because none is strictly decreasing on every rewrite. A correct measure would need to assign different weights to `app` vs. `recDelta` (or combine lexicographically with a secondary measure on the third argument of `recDelta`) to compensate for the duplication of `s`. The provided candidates do not achieve this.
