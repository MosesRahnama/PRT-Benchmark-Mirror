## Assessment of the Three Candidate Termination Measures

The file defines a first-order rewrite calculus over a `Trace` type with 8 rewrite rules (`Step`), and proposes three additive natural-number measures `mu1`, `mu2`, `mu3`. A valid termination measure must strictly decrease across every rule. I evaluate each rule for each candidate below.

### The critical failure: `R_rec_succ`

All three candidates fail on `R_rec_succ`. The RHS is `app s (recDelta b s n)`, which **duplicates** the subterm `s` â€” it appears both as the first argument to `app` and again inside the recursive `recDelta`. Under any additive measure $\mu$:

$$\mu(\text{LHS}) = c_r + \mu(b) + \mu(s) + c_\delta + \mu(n)$$
$$\mu(\text{RHS}) = c_a + \mu(s) + c_r + \mu(b) + \mu(s) + \mu(n)$$
$$\Delta = c_\delta - c_a - \mu(s)$$

Since $\mu(s)$ can be arbitrarily large, no **fixed** choice of constructor weights $c_\delta, c_a$ can make $\Delta > 0$ for all `s`. This is a structural impossibility for any simple additive $\mathbb{N}$-valued measure â€” not just these three.

---

### Candidate-by-candidate verdict

**`mu1`** â€” Uniform weight 1 for most constructors, boosted `eqW` weight of 3.
- Covers 7 of 8 rules. Fails `R_rec_succ` with gap $= -\mu_1(s)$ (non-decreasing when $s = \texttt{void}$, strictly increasing otherwise).
- Margin on `R_eq_diff` is tight (gap = 1); the eqW weight of 3 is the minimum that works since `integrate(merge a b)` costs exactly $1+1+\mu(a)+\mu(b) = 2+\mu(a)+\mu(b)$.

**`mu2`** â€” Inflated weights: `integrate` and `merge` at 2, `recDelta` and `eqW` at 5.
- Covers the same 7 of 8 rules. The extra weight budget buys larger margins on every passing rule but **does not help** with `R_rec_succ` (gap is still $-\mu_2(s)$). The inflated constants are wasted; they cannot overcome a deficit that grows with term size.
- `R_eq_diff` margin is still only 1 ($5 - 2 - 2 = 1$), showing that the weight inflation is not even uniformly beneficial.

**`mu3`** â€” Swaps emphasis: `app` gets weight 2 while `recDelta` drops to 1.
- **Strictly worse** on `R_rec_succ`: gap $= 1 - 2 - \mu_3(s) = -1 - \mu_3(s)$, meaning the measure increases even when $s = \texttt{void}$. The other two candidates at least break even at $s = \texttt{void}$.
