Based on an analysis of the provided Lean 4 calculus and the three candidate measures (`mu1`, `mu2`, `mu3`), **all three candidates fail to be valid termination measures**. 

Here is the assessment of the proposed measures:

### 1. The Variable Duplication Flaw (Rule `R_rec_succ`)
None of the three measures are valid because they cannot handle the variable duplication in the `R_rec_succ` rule:
`Step (recDelta b s (delta n)) (app s (recDelta b s n))`

All three candidate measures are simple additive polynomials where every recursive sub-element has a coefficient of `1` (e.g., $M(a) + M(b) + \text{constant}$). 
In `R_rec_succ`, the variable `s` appears **once on the LHS** but **twice on the RHS** (once inside `app`, once inside the recursive `recDelta`). 

Because `s` can be an arbitrarily large `Trace` term, its weight $\mu(s)$ is unbounded. The difference $\mu(LHS) - \mu(RHS)$ results in the form $\text{Constant} - \mu(s)$, which will always be negative for a sufficiently large `s`. Linear additive measures cannot prove termination for rules where a variable is duplicated. 

### 2. Behavior on the Remaining Rules
If we were to exclude `R_rec_succ`, all three candidate measures successfully prove strictly decreasing weights for the rest of the calculus functions:
* **`R_eq_diff` (`eqW a b -> integrate (merge a b)`)**:
   * **`mu1`**: $LHS = 3$, $RHS = 1 (\text{int}) + 1 (\text{merge}) = 2$. $(3 > 2)$ - **Valid**
   * **`mu2`**: $LHS = 5$, $RHS = 2 (\text{int}) + 2 (\text{merge}) = 4$. $(5 > 4)$ - **Valid**
   * **`mu3`**: $LHS = 4$, $RHS = 2 (\text{int}) + 1 (\text{merge}) = 3$. $(4 > 3)$ - **Valid**
* **`R_merge_cancel` (`merge t t -> t`)**: 
   Since all measures compute $C_{merge} + 2\mu(t)$ on the LHS and $\mu(t)$ on the RHS, they easily decrease.

### Summary
To prove termination for this calculus, a non-linear polynomial interpretation, Lexicographic Path Ordering (LPO), or a measure that heavily weights the $n$ argument (the inductive parameter of `recDelta`) to outweigh the duplication of $s$ would be required. `mu1`, `mu2`, and `mu3` identically fail the strict decrease requirement for `R_rec_succ`.
