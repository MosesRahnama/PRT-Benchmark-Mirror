In
, **none** of `mu1`, `mu2`, or `mu3` is a valid strict termination measure for `Step`.

| Candidate | Verdict | Failing rule |
|---|---|---|
| `mu1` | invalid | `R_rec_succ` |
| `mu2` | invalid | `R_rec_succ` |
| `mu3` | invalid | `R_rec_succ` |

The obstruction is always the same: `R_rec_succ` duplicates the `s` argument.

For `R_rec_succ`:
```lean
Step (recDelta b s (delta n)) (app s (recDelta b s n))
```

### `mu1`
$$
\mu_1(\text{lhs}) = 2 + \mu_1(b) + \mu_1(s) + \mu_1(n)
$$
$$
\mu_1(\text{rhs}) = 2 + \mu_1(b) + 2\mu_1(s) + \mu_1(n)
$$

So
$$
\mu_1(\text{rhs}) - \mu_1(\text{lhs}) = \mu_1(s).
$$

Thus it is:
- equal when `s = void`,
- larger when `s` has positive measure.

So `mu1` is not strictly decreasing.

### `mu2`
$$
\mu_2(\text{lhs}) = 6 + \mu_2(b) + \mu_2(s) + \mu_2(n)
$$
$$
\mu_2(\text{rhs}) = 6 + \mu_2(b) + 2\mu_2(s) + \mu_2(n)
$$

Hence
$$
\mu_2(\text{rhs}) - \mu_2(\text{lhs}) = \mu_2(s).
$$

Same failure as `mu1`.

### `mu3`
$$
\mu_3(\text{lhs}) = 2 + \mu_3(b) + \mu_3(s) + \mu_3(n)
$$
$$
\mu_3(\text{rhs}) = 3 + \mu_3(b) + 2\mu_3(s) + \mu_3(n)
$$

Hence
$$
\mu_3(\text{rhs}) - \mu_3(\text{lhs}) = 1 + \mu_3(s),
$$
so it **always increases** on `R_rec_succ`.

### Concrete counterexample
Take `b = void`, `s = delta void`, `n = void`.

Then:
- `mu1`: `3 -> 4`
- `mu2`: `7 -> 8`
- `mu3`: `3 -> 5`

### Overall assessment
- All three measures do decrease on the other seven rules.
- But a single non-decreasing rule is enough to invalidate a ranking function.
- So the correct classification is:

**`mu1`: reject.**  
**`mu2`: reject.**  
**`mu3`: reject.**
