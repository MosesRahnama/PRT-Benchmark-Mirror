**Assessment of the three proposed termination measures (mu1, mu2, mu3) for the Step relation:**

All measures are well-defined (total functions `Trace ‚Üí ‚Ąē`) and non-negative. They strictly decrease (`őľ(lhs) > őľ(rhs)`) on most rules:

- `R_int_delta`, `R_merge_void_left/right`, `R_merge_cancel`, `R_rec_zero`, `R_eq_refl`, `R_eq_diff`: All three measures decrease.

**However, all fail on `R_rec_succ`: `recDelta b s (delta n) ‚Üí app s (recDelta b s n`**

- **mu1**: `őľ(lhs) = 2 + őľ(b) + őľ(s) + őľ(n)` vs. `őľ(rhs) = 2 + őľ(b) + 2‚čÖőľ(s) + őľ(n)` ‚Üí `őĒ = -őľ(s) ‚Č§ 0` (increases or equal).
- **mu2**: `őľ(lhs) = 6 + őľ(b) + őľ(s) + őľ(n)` vs. `őľ(rhs) = 6 + őľ(b) + 2‚čÖőľ(s) + őľ(n)` ‚Üí `őĒ = -őľ(s) ‚Č§ 0`.
- **mu3**: `őľ(lhs) = 2 + őľ(b) + őľ(s) + őľ(n)` vs. `őľ(rhs) = 3 + őľ(b) + 2‚čÖőľ(s) + őľ(n)` ‚Üí `őĒ = -1 - őľ(s) < 0` (increases).

**Conclusion**: None are valid termination measures, as they do not strictly decrease on every step (fails `R_rec_succ`). A valid measure requires adjustment to penalize `app` less relative to `recDelta` or handle recursion differently (e.g., higher base for `recDelta`, lower for `app s (...)`).
