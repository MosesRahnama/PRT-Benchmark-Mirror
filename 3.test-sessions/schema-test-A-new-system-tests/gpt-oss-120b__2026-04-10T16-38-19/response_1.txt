**Assessment:** Strong normalization can be proved from the rewrite rules alone.

**Reasoning:**  
- The only rules are  

  1. `F(x, y, Z) → x`  
  2. `F(x, y, S(n)) → G(F(x, y, n))`

- The third argument of `F` is either the constant `Z` or a successor term `Sⁿ(Z)`.  
- Rule 2 strictly decreases the “size” of that argument: `S(n)` is replaced by `n`. The outer `G` wrapper does not introduce any new `S` constructors, so the measure “number of `S` symbols in the third argument of the leftmost `F`” strictly decreases with each use of rule 2.  
- After finitely many applications of rule 2 the argument becomes `Z`, at which point rule 1 applies and eliminates the `F` altogether, yielding the variable `x`.  
- No rule creates new `F` or `S` symbols, and there are no rules for `G`, so once a `G` is introduced the term is in normal form (aside from the inner `F` which is already decreasing).  

Thus every reduction sequence terminates, establishing strong normalization without any additional structural axioms or external assumptions.
