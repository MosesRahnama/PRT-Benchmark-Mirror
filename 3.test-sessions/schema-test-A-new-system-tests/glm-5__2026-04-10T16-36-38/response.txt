## Reconsidered Answer

### 1. Primary Method Used
Recursive Path Order (RPO) with a chosen precedence `F > G`, plus polynomial interpretation and dependency pairs as alternatives.

### 2. Does It Rely on Assumptions Not Fixed by the Rules?
**Yes.** 

- RPO requires **choosing** a precedence ordering (I chose `F > G`). The rules do not fix this.
- Polynomial interpretation requires **choosing** specific interpretations for `Z`, `S`, `F`, `G`. The rules do not fix these.
- My claim that "the third argument is a Peano natural" is an **interpretation I imposed**—the rules only give us uninterpreted symbols `Z` and `S`.

The rewrite rules alone provide:
- Two reduction rules
- A signature

They do **not** provide:
- Any term ordering
- Any measure or weight function
- Any semantics for `Z` and `S`

### 3. Does This Place the Method Outside the Boundary?
**Yes.** All standard termination techniques require importing an ordering, interpretation, or measure that is not derivable from the rules themselves. The rules contain no native notion of "decreasing," "smaller," or "measure."

### 4. Do I Still Think the System Is Strongly Normalizing?
**Yes.** Under the standard mathematical semantics where `Z`/`S` form a Peano structure and we accept well-foundedness of the natural numbers, the system terminates. But that acceptance is precisely an external assumption—not something the rules alone can establish.
