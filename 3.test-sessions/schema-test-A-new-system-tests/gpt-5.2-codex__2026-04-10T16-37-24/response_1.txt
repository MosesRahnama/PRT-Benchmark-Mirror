From the rules alone, strong normalization cannot be established. The system is too underconstrained: only `F` is defined, `G` is otherwise unconstrained, and there are no rules reducing `G(…)` terms. This means you cannot prove termination for all terms purely from these rules, because any term containing `G` is a normal form regardless of size, and there is no global descent argument over all terms. You can show termination for terms that are instances of `F` with a finite `S`-chain (since `F` unfolds to `G`-wrappings and then stops at `Z`), but that does not yield strong normalization for the entire first-order term system without extra structure or assumptions.
