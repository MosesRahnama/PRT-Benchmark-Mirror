**Assessment:** **Yes** — under standard first-order TRS semantics on **finite terms**, strong normalization follows from the rewrite rules alone.

**Why:**
A simple well-founded lexicographic measure suffices:

$$
\mu(t) = \big(\#S(t),\ \#F(t)\big)
$$

where $\#S(t)$ is the total number of `S` symbols in the term, and $\#F(t)$ is the total number of `F` symbols.

- `F(x, y, S(n)) -> G(F(x, y, n))`  
  removes exactly one `S`, so $\#S$ strictly decreases.

- `F(x, y, Z) -> x`  
  removes an `F`, so $\#F$ strictly decreases (and $\#S$ does not increase).

No rule introduces new `S`, and no rule can increase this lexicographic measure. Since $(\mathbb{N}\times\mathbb{N}, <_{\text{lex}})$ is well-founded, there can be no infinite rewrite sequence.

So, **from the presented system alone**, and **without extra structural axioms or object-level assumptions**, the rewrite system is strongly normalizing.
