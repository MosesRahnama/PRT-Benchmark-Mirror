**3. Does this place it outside the boundary?** Yes. The precedence is an external structural assumption. A different precedence could fail to orient the rules; the proof's success depends on a choice not determined by the rewrite system.

**4. Do I still think the system is strongly normalizing?** Yes. The third argument of $F$ is a structural recursion parameter — it decreases from $S(n)$ to $n$ in the only recursive rule, and $Z$ is base. This is visible in the rules. But *certifying* it required importing LPO and a precedence, which are not themselves derivable from the rules.
