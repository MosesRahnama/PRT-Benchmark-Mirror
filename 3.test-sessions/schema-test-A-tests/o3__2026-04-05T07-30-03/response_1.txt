Yes – the two rules are already strongly normalising.  
A standard recursive-path ordering (RPO) proof is enough; no extra structural axioms are needed.

Proof roadmap (sketch)

1. Signature  
   • Function symbols: F/3 , G/2 , S/1 , Z/0.  
   • Variables: x, y, n.

2. Precedence and status  
   Take the strict precedence  
   F  >  G  >  S  >  Z  
   and give F and G lexicographic status; variables are minimal.

3. Decrease of each rule under RPO  
   a) F(x, y, Z) → x     Left-hand side’s top symbol F outranks the variable x ⇒ decrease.  
   b) F(x, y, S(n)) → G(y, F(x, y, n))  

     • Top symbols differ: F > G, so it suffices to show  
       F(x, y, S(n)) > y and F(x, y, S(n)) > F(x, y, n).  
     • First inequality is immediate (variable).  
     • Second: same top symbol F, lexicographic comparison of arguments gives  
       S(n) > n (because S > variable), hence the whole term decreases.

   Both rules are strictly decreasing; therefore the rewrite relation is terminating.

4. Conclusion  
   Termination implies every reduction sequence is finite; thus all terms are strongly normalising with respect to the given rewrite system alone.
