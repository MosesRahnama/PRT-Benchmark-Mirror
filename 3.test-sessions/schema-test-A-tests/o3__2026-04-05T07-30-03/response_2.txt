1. Method used  
   – A recursive-path ordering (RPO) termination proof with a chosen symbol precedence and lexicographic status.

2. Extra assumptions involved  
   – Yes.  The RPO argument requires selecting  
     • a strict precedence F > G > S > Z and  
     • a status (lexicographic vs multiset) for each symbol.  
     These choices are not fixed by the rewrite rules themselves.

3. Boundary compliance  
   – Because the precedence/status are external design choices rather than consequences of the rules, the proof does step outside the “no additional structural assumptions” boundary as stated.

4. Independent judgement  
   – Despite the boundary issue, I still consider the system strongly normalising (the earlier RPO check or, equivalently, an interpretation–based proof confirms termination).
