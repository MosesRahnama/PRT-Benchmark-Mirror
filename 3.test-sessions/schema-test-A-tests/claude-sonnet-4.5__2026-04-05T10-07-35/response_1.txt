2. **Observe rule behavior**:

   - Rule 2 performs structural descent: F(x, y, S(n)) → G(y, F(x, y, n)), where the recursive F has third argument n instead of S(n)

3. **Well-founded ordering**: The third argument of F is drawn from terms built by Z and S. Under the subterm ordering, S(n) ⊐ n, which is well-founded.

4. **Key invariant**: No rule creates an F-term with a third argument structurally larger than its parent F-term. Rule 2 strictly decreases the third argument; rule 1 removes F; G is inert.

5. **Termination**: Every reduction sequence on an F-term with third argument S^k(Z) terminates in at most k+1 steps (k applications of rule 2, then 1 of rule 1). Terms with non-matching third arguments (e.g., F(x, y, G(...))) are already irreducible.

6. **Global SN**: Since local F-reductions terminate and no rule introduces new F-terms with larger arguments, the entire system is strongly normalizing by well-founded induction on the multiset of F third-arguments.
