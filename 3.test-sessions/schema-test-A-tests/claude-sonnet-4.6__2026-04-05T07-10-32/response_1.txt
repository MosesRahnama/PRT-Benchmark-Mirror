## Raw Assessment

**Yes, strong normalization is establishable from these two rules alone, with no imported axioms.**

The system is:
```
F(x, y, Z)    -> x
F(x, y, S(n)) -> G(y, F(x, y, n))
```

**Critical observation:** `G` appears only on right-hand sides. It has no left-hand side rule, so every `G`-headed term is already in normal form. The only redexes in the entire system are `F`-headed.

---

## Proof Roadmap (LPO)

Fix the precedence $F \succ G \succ S \succ Z$ and apply the **Lexicographic Path Order (LPO)**. Both rules orient strictly downward; no external lemmas are needed.

**Rule 1:** $F(x,\, y,\, Z) \succ_{\mathrm{lpo}} x$
- $x$ is a proper subterm of the left-hand side. Subterm property of LPO applies directly. $\checkmark$

**Rule 2:** $F(x,\, y,\, S(n)) \succ_{\mathrm{lpo}} G(y,\, F(x,\, y,\, n))$
- Since $F \succ G$, it suffices to show $F(x,y,S(n)) \succ_{\mathrm{lpo}} t$ for every $t \in \{y,\ F(x,y,n)\}$.
  - $y$ is a proper subterm of the LHS — subterm property. $\checkmark$
  - $F(x,y,n)$ has the same root; compare lexicographically: first two arguments are identical ($x$, $y$), and for the third: $S(n) \succ_{\mathrm{lpo}} n$ because $n$ is a proper subterm of $S(n)$. $\checkmark$

Since LPO is a **reduction ordering** (well-founded, monotone, closed under substitution), every rewrite step strictly decreases the LPO measure. By well-foundedness this rules out infinite chains. $\blacksquare$

---

## Why No Extra Axioms Are Needed

- LPO is a purely syntactic, rule-structural criterion. Orientation of both rules is verified by inspecting the rule shapes alone.
- The universal quantification over $x, y$ (arbitrary terms) is handled automatically: LPO is closed under all substitutions, so the ordering checked at the variable level transfers to all ground instances.
- No induction schema, no arithmetic axioms, no structural assumptions about `S` or `Z` beyond their being constructors (no rules) are imported.

The system is therefore **strongly normalizing on its own terms**.
