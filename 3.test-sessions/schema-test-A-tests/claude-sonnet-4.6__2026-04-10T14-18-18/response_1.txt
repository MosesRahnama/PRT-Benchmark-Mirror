```
(VAR x y n)
(RULES
```

**Reasoning:**

1. **G has no rules.** It appears only on right-hand sides and never as a left-hand side head. In a standard first-order TRS this means G is a constructor — all G-rooted terms are in normal form and cannot be further reduced.

2. **F recurses strictly on its third argument.** Rule 2 maps `F(x, y, S(n))` to a term containing `F(x, y, n)`. The third argument goes from `S(n)` to `n`, which is a strict subterm of `S(n)`. By well-foundedness of the subterm ordering on finite terms — a purely meta-theoretic fact about first-order term algebras, not an imported object-level axiom — this descent terminates.

3. **The base case is trivially terminating.** Rule 1 eliminates `F` entirely in one step.

4. **No other redexes exist.** Z and S have no rules; the only reducible function symbol is F.

**Proof sketch:** By structural induction on the third argument of F. The measure `|third arg|` (depth of S-nesting) strictly decreases at each application of Rule 2, reaching the base Rule 1 in finitely many steps. After each F-step, the result is either a variable (Rule 1) or a G-term whose only potentially reducible subterm is the recursively produced F-term with a smaller counter. The induction closes without requiring any object-level axioms about arithmetic, ordinals, or external structure.
