## Raw Assessment

**Yes, strong normalization can be established from the presented rules alone.**

The system has two rules and one defined symbol:

```
F(x, y, Z)    -> x                    (base)
F(x, y, S(n)) -> G(y, F(x, y, n))    (step)
```

`G`, `Z`, `S` are constructors (no left-hand sides). `F` is the sole defined symbol.

---

## Why It Is Strongly Normalizing

The third argument of every `F`-redex is a unary natural number. Rule (step) replaces `S(n)` with `n` — a strict structural decrease — while rule (base) eliminates `F` entirely. No rule introduces a new `F`-symbol with a third argument that is larger than or equal to the original, and no rule introduces `F` anywhere except in the (step) RHS with a strictly smaller third argument.

---

## Proof Roadmap — Polynomial Interpretation

Define a weakly monotone polynomial interpretation over $\mathbb{N}$:

$$
[Z] = 0,\quad [S(n)] = [n]+1,\quad [G(y,z)] = [y]+[z]
$$
$$
[F(x,y,n)] = (x + y + 1)(n + 1)
$$

**Check rule 1** $F(x,y,Z) \to x$:

$$
(x+y+1)(0+1) = x+y+1 \;>\; x \qquad \checkmark \quad (\text{since } y \ge 0)
$$

**Check rule 2** $F(x,y,S(n)) \to G(y, F(x,y,n))$:

$$
(x+y+1)(n+2) \;\stackrel{?}{>}\; y + (x+y+1)(n+1)
$$
$$
\Leftrightarrow\; (x+y+1)(n+1) + (x+y+1) > y + (x+y+1)(n+1)
$$
$$
\Leftrightarrow\; x+y+1 > y \;\Leftrightarrow\; x+1 > 0 \qquad \checkmark
$$

Both inequalities are strict for all values of $x, y, n \in \mathbb{N}$. By the standard **polynomial interpretation method** (Lankford 1979), this certifies that every rewrite sequence is finite, i.e., the TRS is **strongly normalizing**.

---

## No External Axioms Needed

The proof uses only:
1. The two rules as written.
2. The polynomial interpretation (a syntactic termination technique applied to the rule signatures).
3. No object-level induction axioms, no imported structural results — the decreasing measure is entirely derivable from the rule shapes.
