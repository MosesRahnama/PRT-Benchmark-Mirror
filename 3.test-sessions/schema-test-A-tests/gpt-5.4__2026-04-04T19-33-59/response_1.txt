Raw assessment: yes. Strong normalization can be established from the rewrite system alone, with no need to import structural axioms or object-level assumptions.

Short roadmap:
1. Use a standard simplification ordering such as LPO or RPO with precedence F > G > S > Z.
2. Orient the first rule immediately, since F(x, y, Z) is strictly greater than the variable x.
3. Orient the second rule by noting:
   F(x, y, S(n)) > F(x, y, n) because the third argument strictly decreases from S(n) to n.
   F(x, y, S(n)) > y as well.
   Since F > G, this yields F(x, y, S(n)) > G(y, F(x, y, n)).

That gives a well-founded decrease on every rewrite step, so there is no infinite reduction sequence. In other words, the TRS is strongly normalizing on its own.

The key reason this works is that the only recursive call is on a strictly smaller third argument, and G is inert here: it introduces no new rewrite rule and does not increase the ordering.
