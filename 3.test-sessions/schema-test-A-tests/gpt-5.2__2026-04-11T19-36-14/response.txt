4. Separately: yes, I still think the TRS is strongly normalizing. A direct rule-derived measure works: take the multiset of “\(S\)-depth of the 3rd argument” over all occurrences of \(F(\_,\_,\_)\). Rule 1 removes an \(F\) occurrence; rule 2 replaces one \(F(\_,\_,S(t))\) by \(F(\_,\_,t)\) (depth strictly decreases by 1) and does not introduce any new \(F\) occurrences. No rule can ever create an \(S(\cdot)\) at the top of a subterm that wasn’t already there. So every rewrite step strictly decreases that multiset in a well-founded way, ruling out infinite reductions.
