Yes—based on the rewrite rules alone, strong normalization is establishable.

Reasoning (brief): every rewrite step either
- eliminates an `F(..., ..., Z)` redex directly (`F(x,y,Z) -> x`), or
- transforms `F(x,y,S(n))` into `G(y, F(x,y,n))`, where the recursive `F` call has strictly smaller third argument (one `S` removed).

There is no rule that rewrites `G`, `S`, or `Z`, and no rule that can re-introduce an extra `S` in that recursive third-argument position. So recursive `F`-chains strictly descend and must terminate; reductions in subterms are also finite for the same reason. Hence no infinite rewrite sequence is generated by this TRS alone (without extra axioms like AC/associativity/etc.).
