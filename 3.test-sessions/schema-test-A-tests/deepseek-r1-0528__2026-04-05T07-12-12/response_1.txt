## Raw Assessment

**Yes — strong normalization is directly provable from these two rules alone.** No imported axioms are needed.

---

## The System

```
(VAR x y n)
(RULES
  F(x, y, Z)    -> x
  F(x, y, S(n)) -> G(y, F(x, y, n))
)
```

- F is the only defined symbol. G, S, Z have no rules — they are constructors/irreducible.
- Every rewrite step fires exactly one F-redex.

---

## Proof Roadmap

**Method 1 — Polynomial Interpretation (simplest)**

Define the weight of a term as the sum, over all F-subterms F(a, b, c), of `(|c| + 1)` where `|c|` is the size of the third argument.

| Rule | LHS weight contribution | RHS weight contribution | Change |
|------|------------------------|------------------------|--------|
| F(x, y, Z) → x | `|Z| + 1 = 2` | 0 (F disappears) | −2 |
| F(x, y, S(n)) → G(y, F(x, y, n)) | `|S(n)| + 1 = |n| + 2` | `|n| + 1` | −1 |

The variables x, y, n may carry their own F-subterms, but those are **shared** between LHS and RHS and cancel. The weight is a non-negative integer that drops by at least 1 on every step; termination follows from well-foundedness of $\mathbb{N}$.

**Method 2 — Recursive Path Ordering (clean alternative)**

Set precedence `F > G > S > Z` with lexicographic argument comparison for F.

- **Rule 1:** `x` is a proper subterm of the LHS, so `x <_{\text{rpo}} F(x,y,Z)`. ✓  
- **Rule 2:** `G < F`, so `G(y, F(x,y,n)) <_{\text{rpo}} F(x,y,S(n))` provided both arguments of G are smaller:
  - `y` is a subterm of the LHS. ✓  
  - `F(x,y,n) <_{\text{rpo}} F(x,y,S(n))`: same head, lex comparison reaches the third position where `n` is a proper subterm of `S(n)`. ✓

Both methods are self-contained. No structural axioms, no object-level assumptions, no auxiliary lemmas beyond the definitions of `|·|` or the standard RPO ordering.

---

## Why There Is No Obstacle

The third argument of every F-redex is a natural number term (built from Z and S), and it decreases by exactly one constructor per call. G is strictly irreducible, so it cannot create new redexes. The system has no critical pairs and no overlap. SN is unconditional.
