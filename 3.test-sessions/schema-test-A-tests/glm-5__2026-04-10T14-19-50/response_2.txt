## Reconsidered Answer

**1. Primary method used:**
A size measure (interpretation function) counting `S` constructors in the third argument of `F`, combined with the well-foundedness of the natural number ordering.

**2. Does it rely on assumptions not fixed by the rules themselves?**
**Yes.** The method imports:
- An interpretation function mapping terms to natural numbers
- The well-foundedness of the natural number ordering

Neither the interpretation nor the well-foundedness is derivable from the rewrite rules alone—they are external mathematical structures imposed on the system.

**3. Does this place the method outside the stated boundary?**
**Yes.** Well-foundedness of an ordering is a structural axiom. The rules define syntactic transformations but do not establish any well-founded ordering. Importing the natural number ordering's well-foundedness violates the constraint against importing structural axioms.

**4. Do I still think the system is strongly normalizing?**
**Yes.** The system is almost certainly strongly normalizing by standard termination analysis. But establishing this *formally* from the rules alone—without importing well-foundedness, an interpretation, or an ordering—cannot be done within the stated boundary. The boundary requires the proof to use only what is derivable from the rules, and well-foundedness is not derivable from syntactic rewrite rules.
