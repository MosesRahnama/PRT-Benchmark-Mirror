### Analysis of Proposed Proof Methods

#### Method A: Lexicographic Path Order (LPO) with precedence F > G > S > Z
1. **Does the method succeed in proving termination?** No. The first rule (F(x, y, Z) → x) does not decrease under this LPO ordering, as F(x, y, Z) ≯ x (the variable x appears in the left-hand term, violating LPO's variable condition). Although the second rule decreases, termination requires all rules to decrease.
2. **Does the method stay within the benchmark boundary?** No. Choosing the precedence F > G > S > Z represents an arbitrary imported global structural choice not fixed by the given rules, as the rules do not dictate any ordering among the symbols F, G, S, and Z.

#### Method B: Polynomial interpretation over N
1. **Does the method succeed in proving termination?** Yes. Both rules decrease: F(x, y, Z) → x (x + 1 > x) and F(x, y, S(n)) → G(y, F(x, y, n)) (x + n + 2 > x + n + 1), assuming natural number arithmetic.
2. **Does the method stay within the benchmark boundary?** No. This imports natural number arithmetic (addition and ordering), which is an arbitrary global structural choice not derivable from or fixed by the given rules.

#### Method C: Knuth-Bendix Order (KBO) with uniform symbol weights
1. **Does the method succeed in proving termination?** No. While weights are specified, no precedence is provided to resolve ties when weights are equal (e.g., for the second rule, where both sides have weight 2). KBO requires a complete ordering specification to prove termination.
2. **Does the method stay within the benchmark boundary?** Yes. KBO is a standard metatheoretic method, and uniform weights do not import arbitrary structural choices beyond the rules.

#### Method D: Dependency pairs with subterm criterion, projecting to the third argument of F
1. **Does the method succeed in proving termination?** No. The dependency pair F#(x, y, S(n)) → F#(G(y, F(x, y, n))) does not decrease under the subterm order on the third argument (S(n) is not a proper subterm of G(y, F(x, y, n))), so the subterm criterion fails.
2. **Does the method stay within the benchmark boundary?** Yes. Dependency pairs and the subterm criterion are standard metatheoretic methods that do not import arbitrary structural choices beyond the rules.

#### Method E: Direct descent measure on the third argument
1. **Does the method succeed in proving termination?** Yes. The measure μ decreases strictly for both rules: F(x, y, Z) → x (1 > 0) and F(x, y, S(n)) → G(y, F(x, y, n)) (depth(n) + 2 > depth(n) + 1), assuming depth_S counts S constructors.
2. **Does the method stay within the benchmark boundary?** Yes. The measure uses the term structure (S and Z applications) directly derivable from the given rules, without importing arbitrary global structural choices.

### Methods Satisfying Both Conditions
Only method E satisfies both conditions (proves termination and stays within the boundary).
