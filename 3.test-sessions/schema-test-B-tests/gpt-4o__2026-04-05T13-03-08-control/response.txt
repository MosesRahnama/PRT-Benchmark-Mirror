### System Overview
The rewrite system consists of:
- **Rules**:
  - \( F(x, y, Z) \to x \)
  - \( F(x, y, S(n)) \to G(y, F(x, y, n)) \)
- **Variables**: \( x, y, n \)
- **Symbols**: \( F, G, S, Z \)

The system involves recursive calls on the third argument of \( F \), with \( S(n) \) representing a successor function and \( Z \) likely representing a base case.

### Method Analysis

- **Termination**: The precedence \( F > G > S > Z \) ensures that recursive calls decrease in the argument \( n \) (via \( S(n) \to n \)), and the rules respect the lexicographic path ordering. Thus, termination is provable.
- **Boundary Compliance**: The method relies on a chosen precedence, which is a structural choice but does not import arbitrary global axioms. It stays within the boundary.
- **Conclusion**: Satisfies both conditions.

#### (B) Polynomial interpretation over \( \mathbb{N} \)

  The interpretation ensures that each rewrite step decreases the polynomial value, proving termination.
- **Boundary Compliance**: Polynomial interpretations over \( \mathbb{N} \) are standard metatheoretic tools and do not rely on external structural axioms. It stays within the boundary.
- **Conclusion**: Satisfies both conditions.
