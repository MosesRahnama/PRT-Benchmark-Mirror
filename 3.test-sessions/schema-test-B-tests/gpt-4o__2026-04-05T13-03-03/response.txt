To analyze the five proposed proof methods (A-E) for the given term rewriting system, I will evaluate each method based on the two conditions:

1. **Does the method succeed in proving termination of the system as stated?**
2. **Does the method stay within the original benchmark boundary?**

### System Overview
The rewrite system consists of:
- **Variables**: `x`, `y`, `n`
- **Rules**:
  1. `F(x, y, Z) -> x`
  2. `F(x, y, S(n)) -> G(y, F(x, y, n))`

The challenge is to prove termination (strong normalization) without importing structural axioms or assumptions beyond what is derivable from the given rules.

---

### Method Analysis

#### (A) Path-order argument of the LPO/RPO kind with precedence `F > G > S > Z`
- **Termination**: The precedence `F > G > S > Z` aligns with the rules, as `F` reduces to `G` and `S` reduces to `Z`. This method can prove termination.
- **Boundary Compliance**: LPO/RPO arguments rely only on the rewrite rules and precedence, staying within the benchmark boundary.
- **Conclusion**: Satisfies both conditions.

---

#### (B) Polynomial interpretation over ℕ
- **Termination**: The interpretation `[F(x, y, n)] = x + n + 1` and `[S(n)] = n + 1` ensures that each rewrite step decreases the polynomial value, proving termination.
- **Boundary Compliance**: Polynomial interpretations over ℕ are external constructs and introduce assumptions about the structure of ℕ, which are not derivable from the given rules.
- **Conclusion**: Fails the boundary condition.

---

#### (C) Knuth-Bendix Order (KBO) with uniform symbol weights
- **Termination**: Uniform weights `w(F) = w(G) = w(S) = w(Z) = 1` fail to distinguish between the symbols' roles in the rewrite rules. This method cannot prove termination.
- **Boundary Compliance**: KBO itself stays within the benchmark boundary, but it does not succeed in proving termination.
- **Conclusion**: Fails the termination condition.

---

#### (D) Dependency pairs with subterm criterion
- **Termination**: Dependency pairs project the third argument of `F`, analyzing the recursive structure of `S(n)`. This method can prove termination.
- **Boundary Compliance**: Dependency pairs rely only on the rewrite rules and subterm projections, staying within the benchmark boundary.
- **Conclusion**: Satisfies both conditions.

---

#### (E) Direct descent measure on the third argument
- **Termination**: The measure `mu(F(x, y, n)) = depth_S(n) + 1` strictly decreases with each rewrite step, proving termination.
- **Boundary Compliance**: The descent measure is derived directly from the rewrite rules, staying within the benchmark boundary.
- **Conclusion**: Satisfies both conditions.

---

### Final Result
The methods that satisfy both conditions are:
- **(A) Path-order argument of the LPO/RPO kind**
- **(D) Dependency pairs with subterm criterion**
- **(E) Direct descent measure on the third argument**
