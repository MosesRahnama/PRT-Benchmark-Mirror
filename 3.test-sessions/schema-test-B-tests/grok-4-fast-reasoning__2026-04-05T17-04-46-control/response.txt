### Analysis of Proposed Proof Methods

The term rewriting system (TRS) is defined over variables \(x, y, n\) with rules:
- \( F(x, y, Z) \to x \)
- \( F(x, y, S(n)) \to G(y, F(x, y, n)) \)

This TRS terminates (no infinite rewrite sequences possible, as rewrites only apply when the third argument is \(Z\) or \(S\)-headed, leading to finite descent). Below, each method is evaluated strictly as stated.

#### (A) Lexicographic Path Order (LPO) with precedence \( F > G > S > Z \)
1. **Succeeds in proving termination?** Yes. LPO is a monotonic reduction ordering. For rule 1, \( F(x, y, Z) > x \) holds (top \( F \) dominates variables). For rule 2, \( F(x, y, S(n)) > G(y, F(x, y, n)) \) holds (top \( F > G \)). No cycles or non-orienting rules.
2. **Within boundary?** Yes. LPO uses only the symbols and structure from the rules; precedence is a standard metatheoretic choice compatible with the signature, without importing unrelated axioms or assumptions.

#### (B) Polynomial interpretation over \(\mathbb{N}\):
   \[
   \begin{align*}
   [F(x,y,n)] &= x + n + 1, \\
   [S(n)] &= n + 1, \\
   [G(a,b)] &= b, \\
   [Z] &= 0.
   \end{align*}
   \]
1. **Succeeds in proving termination?** Yes. Interpretations map to \(\mathbb{N}\) with strict decrease: Rule 1 gives \([x] + 1 > [x]\); rule 2 gives \([x] + [n] + 2 > [x] + [n] + 1\). Monotonicity holds for all ground instances.
2. **Within boundary?** Yes. The interpretation is defined solely on the function symbols and variables from the rules; it imposes no external structural axioms beyond the derivable behavior (e.g., \(S\) increments, \(Z\) base).

#### (C) Knuth-Bendix Order (KBO) with uniform symbol weights \( w(F) = w(G) = w(S) = w(Z) = 1 \)
1. **Succeeds in proving termination?** No. Weights are equal (2 for both sides of rule 2). Multiset comparison of subterm top symbols requires a precedence \( > \) on symbols (standard for KBO), but none is specified. Without it (or an unnatural one like \( S > F \)), rule 2 does not orient (\( F(x, y, S(n)) \not> G(y, F(x, y, n)) \)). Rule 1 orients by weight (2 > 0), but incompleteness fails the system.
2. **Within boundary?** N/A (does not succeed).

#### (D) Dependency pairs with subterm criterion, projecting to the third argument of F
1. **Succeeds in proving termination?** Yes. The key dependency pair from rule 2 is \( F(x, y, S(n)) \to F(x, y, n) \). Projecting to the third argument treats \( S(n) \to n \) as a strict subterm descent (third argument decreases). No cycles in the dependency graph; rule 1 is non-recursive. Subterm criterion applies, proving local termination and thus overall.
2. **Within boundary?** Yes. Dependency pairs analyze the rules' recursive structure directly; projection to the third argument follows the rules' fixed pattern (recursion on third position), without external assumptions.

#### (E) Direct descent measure on the third argument:
   \[
   \begin{align*}
   \mu(F(x,y,n)) &= \mathrm{depth}_S(n) + 1, \\
   \mu(G(a,b)) &= \mu(b), \\
   \mu(x) &= 0 \quad (\text{variables}),
   \end{align*}
   \]
   with each rewrite strictly decreasing \(\mu\).
1. **Succeeds in proving termination?** Yes. \(\mathrm{depth}_S\) is well-founded (longest \(S\)-chain length). Rule 1: \(\mu = 1 > 0\). Rule 2: \(\mu = \mathrm{depth}_S(n) + 2 > \mathrm{depth}_S(n) + 1 = \mu(G(y, F(x,y,n)))\). No rewrites if third argument is non-\(S\)/\(Z\)-headed; \(\mu\) decreases where rules apply.
2. **Within boundary?** Yes. The measure uses only the rules' structure (third argument as \(S\)/\(Z\)-descending counter); \(\mathrm{depth}_S\) is derivable from the recursive pattern, without imported global choices.
