For the system
$$
F(x,y,Z)\to x,\qquad F(x,y,S(n))\to G(y,F(x,y,n)),
$$
the termination intuition is “descent on the third argument of $F$.” Evaluating the five methods as stated:

1. A: LPO/RPO-style path order with precedence $F > G > S > Z$
Success: Yes. The first rule is immediate, and the second is orientable because the recursive call moves from $S(n)$ to $n$ while the top symbol also drops from $F$ to $G$.
Boundary: No. The proof depends on a chosen global precedence, which is an imported structural choice not fixed by the rewrite rules themselves.

2. B: Polynomial interpretation over $\mathbb N$
Success: Yes. The interpretation gives
$$
[F(x,y,Z)] = x+1 > x = [x],
$$
and
$$
[F(x,y,S(n))] = x+n+2 > x+n+1 = [G(y,F(x,y,n))].
$$
Boundary: No. This uses an externally chosen algebraic interpretation over $\mathbb N$, so it relies on imported global structure.

3. C: KBO with uniform weights $w(F)=w(G)=w(S)=w(Z)=1$
Success: No. Rule 2 fails the KBO variable condition: $y$ occurs once on the left but twice on the right.
Boundary: No. Even aside from failure, KBO with a fixed weight regime is still an imported global ordering choice.

4. D: Dependency pairs with subterm criterion, projecting the third argument of $F$
Success: Yes. The only relevant dependency pair is
$$
F^\#(x,y,S(n)) \to F^\#(x,y,n),
$$
and projecting the third argument gives $S(n)$ on the left and $n$ on the right, so the subterm criterion closes it.
Boundary: Yes. Under your clarified narrow reading, this stays within boundary: it uses standard metatheoretic soundness machinery, but does not depend on arbitrary imported global structural axioms of the kind used in A-C.

5. E: Direct descent measure on the third argument
Success: No, as stated. It works on the redex itself, but not for arbitrary contextual rewrites. Since $\mu(F(x,y,n))$ ignores the first and second arguments, a rewrite inside one of those positions need not decrease $\mu$ for the whole term.
Boundary: Yes. The measure is drawn directly from the rule’s visible third-argument descent and does not require imported global structural choices.

The only method that satisfies both conditions is D.
