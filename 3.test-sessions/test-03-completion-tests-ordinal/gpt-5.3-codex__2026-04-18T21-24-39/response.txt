### 1) `Step.R_rec_succ b s n`

The key shape is: bound `mu (app s (recDelta b s n))` by a finite-height polynomial in $\omega$, then show that polynomial is strictly below the huge principal term coming from `mu (recDelta b s (delta n))`.

```lean
  /-
    Goal shape after simp:
      omega0 ^ (3:Ordinal) * (mu s + 1) +
        omega0 ^ (2:Ordinal) *
          (omega0 ^ (mu n + mu s + (6:Ordinal)) + omega0 * (mu b + 1) + 1 + 1) + 1
      <
      omega0 ^ (omega0 ^ (5:Ordinal) * (mu n + 1) + 1 + mu s + (6:Ordinal)) +
        omega0 * (mu b + 1) + 1

    Strategy:
    1) Prove a coarse upper bound:
         LHS ≤ omega0 ^ (E_small)    (for some E_small around mu n + mu s + C)
    2) Prove E_small < E_big where
         E_big := omega0 ^ 5 * (mu n + 1) + 1 + mu s + 6
    3) Use monotonicity/strictness of opow in exponent to get
         omega0 ^ E_small < omega0 ^ E_big
    4) Then conclude with `lt_of_le_of_lt` and `lt_of_lt_of_le`.
```

### 2) `Step.R_eq_refl a`

```lean
| _, _, Step.R_eq_refl a => by
```

### 3) `Step.R_eq_diff a b`

Core idea: LHS is bounded by roughly $\omega^{8}$-level growth plus coefficients, while RHS is a single principal term $\omega^{\mu a + \mu b + 9}$, so it dominates.

```lean
| _, _, Step.R_eq_diff a b => by
  simp [mu]
  /-
    Goal shape:
      omega0 ^ (4:Ordinal) *
        (omega0 ^ (3:Ordinal) * (mu a + 1) + omega0 ^ (2:Ordinal) * (mu b + 1) + 1 + 1) + 1
      <
      omega0 ^ (mu a + mu b + (9:Ordinal)) + 1

    Strategy:
    1) Bound the parenthesized merge-term by omega0 ^ (mu a + mu b + 8).
    2) Multiply by omega0^4 and absorb into omega0 ^ (mu a + mu b + 8) (or +const).
    3) Show resulting exponent is < mu a + mu b + 9.
    4) Lift via omega-power monotonicity; then add +1 on RHS.
  -/

  let E : Ordinal := mu a + mu b + (9:Ordinal)

  let L : Ordinal :=
```

## What to focus on when filling the `sorry`s

- Build **coarse principal bounds** first (`≤ omega0 ^ (...)`) rather than trying exact normal forms.
- Reuse this pattern repeatedly:
  - `lt_of_le_of_lt`
  - `lt_of_lt_of_le`
  - monotonicity of `+` on the right (`le_add_right`, `le_add_left`)
- For strict steps, reduce to exponent inequalities and then lift through `omega0 ^ _`.
