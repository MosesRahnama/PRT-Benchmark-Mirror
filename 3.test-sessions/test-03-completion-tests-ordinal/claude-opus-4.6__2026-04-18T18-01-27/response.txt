### Summary

| Case | Core difficulty | Key lemma family |
|---|---|---|
| `R_rec_succ` | Exponent $\omega^5\cdot(\mu(n)+1)$ dominates $\mu(n)+\mu(s)+6$ | `opow_lt_opow_right`, omega-absorption |
| `R_eq_refl` | Positivity of $\omega^k + 1$ | `add_pos_right _ one_pos` |
| `R_eq_diff` | $\omega^4 \cdot \omega^7 \cdot c < \omega^{c+9}$ via absorption | `opow_add`, `mul_lt_mul_of_pos_left` |

The `R_eq_refl` case is essentially trivial and should close with a single tactic. The other two require 3–5 intermediate `have` steps each, all following the same pattern: bound sub-expressions by omega-powers, compare exponents, and appeal to `Ordinal.opow_lt_opow_right omega0_pos`.
