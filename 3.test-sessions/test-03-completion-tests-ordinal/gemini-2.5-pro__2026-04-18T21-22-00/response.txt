### `Step.R_eq_diff`

This case proves the decrease for `eqW a b --> integrate (merge a b)`.

**Goal:** `mu (integrate (merge a b)) < mu (eqW a b)`

**LHS:**
`mu(integrate (merge a b))`
`= omega0^4 * (mu(merge a b) + 1) + 1`
`= omega0^4 * ( (omega0^3 * (mu a + 1) + omega0^2 * (mu b + 1) + 1) + 1) + 1`
`= omega0^4 * (omega0^3 * (mu a + 1) + omega0^2 * (mu b + 1) + 2) + 1`

The dominant term is `omega0^4 * omega0^3 * (mu a + 1)`, which is bounded by `omega0^8` if we consider `mu a` to be small.

**RHS:**
`mu(eqW a b) = omega0^(mu a + mu b + 9) + 1`

**Proof Strategy:**
The exponent on the RHS, `mu a + mu b + 9`, is larger than the exponents of the expanded terms on the LHS (which are at most 7). The proof involves showing that the entire LHS expression is bounded by `omega0^(mu a + mu b + 8)`, which is in turn less than the RHS.
